📐
The 20% Gap Rule
One new thing today — learn it before Round 1 starts
📖 Key Words
Prediction
The number of goals you think you will score — written before the match.
Actual
The number of goals you actually scored — recorded after the match.
Gap
How far off your prediction was. We show it as a percent (%).
Variable
A number in your formula that changes — like time, speed, or distance.

📐 The Gap Formula
How to Calculate Gap %
gap = | prediction − actual |
divided by prediction, then × 100
The | | symbols mean "ignore the minus sign" — always a positive number.
🔢 Step-by-Step Example
1
Predicted 5 goals. Scored 3 goals.
2
Find the difference: |5 − 3| = 2
3
Divide by your prediction: 2 ÷ 5 = 0.4
4
Multiply by 100: 0.4 × 100 = 40%
!
40% is bigger than 20% → Write the sentence.

✍️ When Gap > 20% — Write This Sentence
🗣️ The Sentence Frame

"The variable my model missed was _____ because _____."

1
Look at your prediction formula. What numbers did you plug in?
2
Which number was wrong or approximate?
3
That is your variable. Write one sentence. One is enough.
Example sentence
"The variable my model missed was time because our bot ran slower than normal today and I used the speed from yesterday."
