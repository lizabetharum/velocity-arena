What Is a Commissioner Rule?

This morning you drafted a Commissioner proposal. Now understand how the whole system works — it determines whether your current loadout is still legal in Season 2.

The Big Idea

In real sports leagues, a Commissioner sets rules that all teams must follow — rules about how teams can be built, not just how the game is played. In Velocity Arena, your team is the Commissioner. You write a rule. If the league votes it in, it becomes a math constraint every team must satisfy when they build their bot for Season 2.

What does a rule look like? — Three examples
Inequality Rule
"No team can have Speed greater than 7."
In math: Speed ≤ 7. Every team's Speed value must satisfy this. A team with Speed = 8 would be illegal. This rule rewards teams that already built low-Speed, and forces high-Speed teams to rebuild.
Budget Rule
"Power + Endurance must be at least 10 combined."
In math: Power + Endurance ≥ 10. This is a combined constraint — teams have to think about two stats together, not just each one separately. It changes what a "valid" allocation looks like.
Floor Rule
"Every stat must be at least 3."
In math: Speed ≥ 3 AND Endurance ≥ 3 AND Turning ≥ 3 AND Power ≥ 3. This prevents teams from dumping all points into one stat. It forces balanced builds.
The catch

Rules apply to every team — including yours. If your rule passes and your current Season 1 loadout violates it, you have to rebuild. That's why you need to think about this before the vote.
