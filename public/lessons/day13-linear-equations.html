💡 What is a data point?
A data point = one match result. It has two numbers: your stat and your win %.

Example: your team had 8 Speed points and a 60% win rate.
That data point is written as (8, 60).
The stat goes first. Win % goes second.

📈 What is rate of change?
Rate of change = how much your win % goes up for each stat point you add.

Formula: rate of change = win % change ÷ stat change

Math calls this the slope. We use the letter m.

How accurate is your equation?
Use a match you haven't used yet. Compare what your equation predicted vs what actually happened.
What is a residual?
Residual = predicted win % − actual win %

• Residual = 0: your equation was perfect!
• Positive residual: your equation over-predicted (your win % was lower than expected).
• Negative residual: your equation under-predicted (you did better than expected!)
