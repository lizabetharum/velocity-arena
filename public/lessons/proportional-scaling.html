Why squaring multiplies twice
Original
P × P = P²
→
After scaling P by 1.5
(1.5P) × (1.5P)
= 1.5 × 1.5 × P × P
= 2.25 × P²
The 1.5 multiplied itself. You scaled P by 1.5, but squaring means that 1.5 appears twice — once for each P. So the output grows by 1.5 × 1.5 = 2.25×, not 1.5×. That's why quadratic formulas are much more sensitive to stat changes than linear ones.

Reverse check
If charge speed doubled (100% increase), did Power increase by 50%?
Here's the difference
Speed scaled proportionally. Power did not. When you increased Speed by 50%, motor power went up by exactly 50%. When you increased Power by 50%, charge speed went up by 125% — not 50%. That's because charge speed = P², a quadratic formula. The same input scaling produces a much larger output jump.

Why this matters for your proposal: if you argue for raising a stat cap, you need to know which formula it feeds into. Raising a Speed cap is proportional — predictable. Raising a Power cap is not — the effect grows faster than the stat.
Apply it
The Commissioner is proposing raising the Power cap by 2 points above your current stat. Using your numbers, calculate what that would do to your Charge Speed. Then write one sentence telling the Commissioner whether you support the change and why.
