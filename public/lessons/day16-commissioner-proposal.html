How the Objection Window Works
1
A rule passes by majority vote. The Commissioner's proposal gets the required votes.
2
2-minute window opens. Any team can file a mathematical objection — but it must include a specific number and a calculation showing the % impact on formula output.
Example objection
Rule: Turning ≤ 7 (cap at 7)
Our stat: Turning = 10
Before:
Turn Ratio = 0.2 + 10×0.02 = 0.40
After:
Turn Ratio = 0.2 + 7×0.02 = 0.34
Drop = 15% decrease in Turn Ratio
"Our Turn Ratio drops from 0.40 to 0.34 — that's a 15% decrease. That's our objection."
3
Objection is ruled on. The facilitator decides if the objection meets the mathematical standard. "It's unfair" alone doesn't qualify — you need the number.
4
Rule stands or is revised. If a valid objection is upheld, the number in the rule may be adjusted once. Then it locks.
The Objection Formula — memorize this
"If this rule passes, my [stat] formula output drops from [X] to [Y] — that's a [Z]% decrease."
Without all three values — the stat name, both numbers, and the percent — it does not qualify as a mathematical objection.

Three Ball Modes
The Championship rotates all three. Know which stats each one rewards.
Your rule will apply across all three ball modes. A rule that caps Turning, for example, directly weakens teams in Chaos Mode. Think about which ball mode benefits your team before you write your cap or floor.
🎾
Standard Mode
Tennis Ball
Normal weight, predictable roll, moderate bounce. The baseline mode where all four stats contribute. No single stat dominates.
Rewards: Speed Endurance Turning Power
Most relevant formula: Motor Power = Speed × 5
Reaching the ball faster means more scoring opportunities.
🏓
Chaos Mode
Ping Pong Ball — Light, Fast, Unpredictable
The ball bounces unpredictably — it can change direction on contact. Bots that can redirect quickly have a major edge. Straight-line Speed is less useful than the ability to track and re-engage.
Rewards: Turning ★ Speed
Key formula: Turn Ratio = 0.2 + Turning × 0.02
A Turning of 10 → Turn Ratio = 0.2 + 10×0.02 = 0.40
A Turning of 4 → Turn Ratio = 0.2 + 4×0.02 = 0.28
🟣
Heavy Touch Mode
Foam Ball — Slower Roll, Higher Resistance
The ball rolls slower and absorbs contact. Speed matters less — the bot needs enough force to move the ball effectively. Follow-through on contact determines whether the shot reaches the goal.
Rewards: Power ★ Endurance
Key formulas: Charge Speed = Power²  |  Follow-Through = Power × 200ms
A Power of 8 → Charge Speed = 64, Follow-Through = 1600ms
A Power of 4 → Charge Speed = 16, Follow-Through = 800ms
