Power Test Program

This program tests your bot's charge-and-push behavior. You position the bot, press A to arm it, and it waits for a ball to enter range — then charges at full power, makes contact, and drives through for a set duration before reversing out.

Button Controls
Button A

Arm + Charge

Computes stats, then waits for a ball within 70 cm. When detected, pauses briefly and fires the charge sequence.

Button B

Cycle match time

Steps through 0 → 60 → 120 → 180 seconds of simulated match time. Higher values apply stronger endurance decay to every charge.

Charge Phase
  • Runs at effectiveCharge (chargePower reduced by endurance)
  • Polls border sensors + sonar every 20 ms
  • Ends on border hit OR confirmed contact
  • Two consecutive sonar reads ≤ 5 cm = contact
Follow-Through Phase
  • Only runs if contact was made (not border hit)
  • Switches to currentPower (cruise speed)
  • Duration = POWER × 200 ms
  • Still checks border — stops immediately if hit
  • Reverses 300 ms then stops

Contact Detection

The ultrasonic sensor can give a reading of 0 when it's too close to get a clean ping — but a single 0 can be noise. The program confirms contact with two consecutive low reads.

1
Ultrasonic reads ≤ 5 cm — contactCount increments to 1. Not confirmed yet.
2
Next read (20 ms later) is still ≤ 5 cm — contactCount = 2. Contact confirmed, charge loop exits.
✕
If any read is > 5 cm, contactCount resets to 0. The sequence treats it as noise and keeps charging.
Stats & Formulas
Current Power (cruise speed)
currentPower = SPEED × 5 (% motor power)
Then scaled by endurance: currentPower × enduranceFactor. Minimum 15%.
Charge Power
chargePower = min(POWER², 100)
POWER is squared — small increases at high values have a huge effect. POWER=5→25%, POWER=8→64%, POWER=10→100%. Then scaled by enduranceFactor during the charge.
Follow-Through Duration
FOLLOW_THROUGH_MS = POWER × 200 ms
Higher POWER gives both a harder initial hit and a longer sustained push after contact.
Endurance Decay
decayRate = 20 − ENDURANCE
endurancePercent = max(100 − decayRate × time / 40, 20)
Lower ENDURANCE = higher decayRate = faster power loss over time. Floor is 20% — the bot never goes completely dead. ENDURANCE=20 means decayRate=0 — no decay at all.

⚡ POWER is quadratic. Going from POWER=5 to POWER=6 adds 11 percentage points of charge power (36−25=11). Going from POWER=9 to POWER=10 adds 19 points (100−81=19). Every POWER point you add is worth more than the last. Investing heavily in POWER has sharply increasing returns — but costs stat budget that can't go to SPEED or ENDURANCE.

⚠️
TURNING is declared but not used. It's kept for consistency with other programs but has zero effect in this test. Don't allocate points to it expecting a benefit here.

How to Use This Program
  1. 1 Set your stats in the code. Edit SPEED, ENDURANCE, POWER, and TURNING at the bottom of the file. They must total 20. Re-flash the micro:bit.
  2. 2 Press B to set match time. If you want to test late-match behavior, press B to cycle to 120 s or 180 s before arming.
  3. 3 Position the bot on the field, facing the target zone. Leave clear space ahead — it will charge straight forward.
  4. 4 Press A to arm. The dot appears. Roll the ball toward the bot from ahead until it's within 70 cm.
  5. 5 Watch the sequence. Circle → C → F (or B) → R → OK. Check how far the ball moved and whether the bot stopped cleanly.
⛔
This program charges on detection. Once you press A and a ball (or any object) enters 70 cm range, the bot will drive forward at full charge power. Keep hands and feet clear of its path.
