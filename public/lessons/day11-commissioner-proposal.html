Three Rules for Writing Your Rule
✓
A rule may cap a stat or set a floor.
"No team may have Speed above 10" — allowed
✗
A rule may NOT force a specific value.
"All teams must set Power to exactly 5" — not allowed
Why? It removes team agency — only one correct loadout.
✗
A rule may NOT change formulas or the 20-point budget.
"Teams get 25 points instead of 20" — not allowed
Why? Budget and formulas are the foundation of the game.
Draft Your Rule
Three steps. Your rule builds automatically as you go.
1 Pick a stat to regulate

Your rule will apply to this stat for every team — including yours. Pick the one where the data shows the biggest unfairness.

How to choose — look at your dashboard
Find the biggest gap
Which stat is most different between the top team and the last-place team? That's where the unfairness is.
Too high or too low?
One team too high? → Use a CAP to limit the ceiling.  Last-place team too low? → Use a FLOOR to raise the minimum.
Check: does it actually help last place?
If the last-place team also has a high value in that stat, capping it hurts them too. Make sure your rule gives the struggling team a real advantage.

Back Your Rule with Data
Your proposal needs two data points. For Speed and Power, you must compute the formula output — not just the raw stat. That's your scaling evidence.
Use your proportional scaling calculations
From this morning: Speed × 5 = Motor Power (proportional). Power² = Charge Speed (not proportional). Your rule is stronger when you show what the formula produces at the limit value — not just what the stat is.
