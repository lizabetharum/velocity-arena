From Formula to Equation

Your P(t) formula is already linear — let's write it in slope-intercept form.

The Endurance Decay Formula
P(t) = 100 − (20 − E) × 1.5 × t
P(t) = motor power %  ·  t = time in minutes  ·  E = your Endurance stat

This is already a linear equation. You've seen linear equations in math class written as y = mx + b. Let's rewrite P(t) in that exact form — same equation, different look.

Rewrite in slope-intercept form — click to reveal each step
  1. 1
    P(t) = 100 − (20 − E) × 1.5 × t
    Start with the original formula. Notice: 100 is at the front, and the variable term (with t) is at the end.
  2. 2
    P(t) = −[(20 − E) × 1.5] × t + 100
    Group the coefficient: the term being multiplied by t is negative. We flip the subtraction into a negative slope, and move the constant +100 to the end.
  3. 3
    y = m · x + b
    Match to y = mx + b:  m = −(20 − E) × 1.5 (the slope),  b = 100 (the y-intercept).
    y = P(t) and x = t.
💡

The y-intercept is always 100. Why? Because at t = 0, your bot just started the match — it's always at full power (100%). No matter what your E value is, every bot starts at 100%.

Find the X-Intercept

When does your bot run out of power completely?

The x-intercept is where y = 0 — when P(t) hits zero. That's the moment your bot's power runs out completely. In Season 2 planning, this number tells you how long your bot can survive at full operation.

Solving for t when P(t) = 0
0 = m · t + 100
−100 = m · t (subtract 100 from both sides)
t = −100 / m (divide by m — m is negative, so t is positive!)
t = 100 / ((20 − E) × 1.5)
Worked Example · E = 6
m = −(20 − 6) × 1.5 = −14 × 1.5 = −21
t = 100 / ((20 − 6) × 1.5) = 100 / (14 × 1.5) = 100 / 21 ≈ 4.76 minutes
A bot with E = 6 would theoretically run out of power at 4.76 minutes.
