Math Patterns

How the Math Works

Six math patterns power your bot. See each one with a graph and a question.

Unit Rate
Speed → Motor Power

A unit rate means one input always produces the same fixed output — in this case, every +1 Speed gives exactly +5% motor power. The rate is constant: 5% per Speed point.

Linear Function
A Straight Line on a Graph

When you plot Speed vs. Motor Power, you get a straight line. That's the definition of a linear function — constant slope, no curves. Every point on the line follows the same rule: y = 5x.

Linear Decay
Endurance → Power Over Time

Linear decay is a straight line going down. P(t) = 100 − (20 − E) × 1.5 × t. Low Endurance makes the slope steeper — the bot loses power faster. High Endurance means a shallower drop.

Nonlinear Function
Power → Charge (P²)

The Power formula is nonlinear: charge = P². Doubling P doesn't double charge — it quadruples it. The graph curves upward. Compare it to the straight Speed line.

Proportional Scaling
Does Doubling the Input Double the Output?

Proportional means input × k → output × k. Speed is proportional — double it, double motor power. Power is not proportional — double P, and charge quadruples.

Ratio
Comparing Stats to Each Other

A ratio compares two numbers. S:E = 8:4 = 2:1 — your bot is twice as fast as it has endurance. Ratios reveal how you built your robot and what its strengths are.
