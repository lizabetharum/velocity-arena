About Velocity Arena.

Four weeks. One robot. Real math. A summer math camp for rising 9th graders, run in NYC by NYC FIRST and supported by XQ Institute Summer Sparks.

The program

OVERVIEW

What is Velocity Arena?

A 4-week summer math camp for rising 9th graders run by NYC FIRST. Students build and program BBC micro:bit v2 robots to compete in challenges — and every challenge requires real math to solve.

The program bridges algebra and physical computing, giving students a concrete reason to care about variables, equations, and functions. Math is not taught in isolation — it shows up as the tool you need to make your robot win.

Instructional approach

PEDAGOGY

How we teach

Velocity Arena runs on a daily rhythm, not a single instructional model. Each 6-hour day moves through five distinct modes: a Hook activity that builds trust and primes thinking, a Challenge Block where teams grapple with a math problem before the facilitator explains anything, self-directed Open Lab time for iteration, Match-Build-Make where math gets tested against a real robot on a real course, and the ENDS closing ritual that closes the day's arc.

The Challenge Block is where most explicit math instruction lives. Facilitators launch in under 5 minutes, then step back. Teams grapple for 20–25 minutes before any whole-class share-out.

Assessment is embedded in performance, not administered separately. The data dashboard each team maintains across all four weeks — tracking match outcomes, speed allocations, predictions versus results — is the primary artifact of learning. The stat card each student carries from Day 1 makes every math decision visible and binding.
