00Roadmap

4-Week Velocity Arena Roadmap.

One-page overview of the 20-day arc. Each week shows the core math, what students produce, what you must do, and what to watch for.

WEEK 01
Stats, formulas, and bot behavior
  • Core mathProportional relationships, percent, rate of change, and basic coordinate ideas through Speed, Endurance, Turning, and Power formulas.
  • Student workStat cards with a 20-point allocation, formula worksheets (Speed, Endurance, Power), early team dashboards, and written one-sentence explanations using sentence frames.
  • You preparePrinted stat cards, Day 1 Pre-Task Diagnostic, Speed/Endurance/Power worksheets, visible "Math Vocabulary Wall" space, and an Operations Quick Reference at each station.
  • Routines to establishDaily launch → formula on board with one worked example → 30-40 minutes of team Challenge Block work with visible time checkpoints → 5-10 minute share-out and ENDS prompt.
  • Watch forCampers stuck on operations (division, squaring, order of operations), ELLs leaving writing prompts blank, and advanced campers finishing calculations 10-15 minutes early.
  • Key movesQuietly point to Operations Quick Reference (no reteach mini-lesson), add sentence frames to written prompts, and give early finishers extension cards tied to the same formulas.
WEEK 02
Graphs, distance, and match strategy
  • Core mathScatter plots, linear relationships between stats and goals, and distance formula on the 8×4 field grid.
  • Student workClass Speed vs. Goals scatter plot, card-sorts matching situations, tables, graphs, and equations, and distance-formula calculations from tip-off and actual match coordinates.
  • You preparePrinted graph sets (verbal / table / graph / equation), large field grid on the floor or board, distance-formula worksheets, and updated dashboard access for match data.
  • Routines to reinforce"Subtract, square, add, square root" chant for distance formula, grid-check verification (students count diagonals to check their d), and structured share-outs that ask "What does this slope/distance mean in bot terms?".
  • Watch forStudents swapping x and y, mis-reading decimals, or treating every relationship as proportional. Highlight teams whose actual goals do not match a proportional prediction.
  • Key movesUse sentence frames for "line of best fit" interpretations, assign Model Tracker or Checker roles to advanced campers, and prompt ELLs to use visuals and the vocabulary wall when explaining graphs.
WEEK 03
Inequalities, linear models, and proposals
  • Core mathSystems of inequalities for valid stat loadouts, building and testing linear equations (y = mx + b) from real dashboard data, and percent change across seasons.
  • Student workTables of valid loadouts under multiple constraints, handwritten equations with labeled slope and intercept from two matches, residual tables comparing predicted and actual win probabilities, and draft commissioner proposals.
  • You prepareConstraint tables, proposal worksheets with required data points, percent-change worksheets using Season 1 vs. Season 2 stats, and clear norms for match-day data logging.
  • Routines to reinforce"Build equation from two real points → test on three new points → read residuals aloud," and using the dashboard as the single source of truth for any number cited in a proposal.
  • Watch forStudents switching x and y in slope, treating a single outlier as proof, or proposing rules that mainly benefit their own team instead of the last-place team.
  • Key movesUse the Verification worksheet to force "claim → dashboard location → verified number," have one camper per team ready to explain a calculation from memory, and assign a Math Verifier role during the Commissioners Meeting.
WEEK 04
Refinement, explanation, and exhibition
  • Core mathRevisiting percent change, re-allocating stats based on evidence, and turning data and formulas into clear explanations for visitors and the final diagnostic.
  • Student workFinalized stat configurations, revised proposals after conflict-of-interest checks, ENDS journal reflections making sense of strategy, and visitor teaching tasks at exhibition.
  • You preparePost-Task Diagnostic access, exhibition stations with simple visual explanation cards, and a clear checklist for what each team must have ready (bot, dashboard, one explanation card, one spokesperson).
  • Routines to reinforce"Data first, story second" norm: any claim about improvement must point to a specific dashboard number or worksheet calculation; one camper per team can explain a key formula aloud.
  • Watch forTeams revising stories without revising numbers, or ELL campers whose math is strong but verbal explanation is blocked; use visual explanation cards and sentence frames to support them.
  • Key movesAllow journals in a first language with a short English summary, prepare one visual explanation per ELL camper, and keep exhibition assessment focused on whether visitors can complete the task independently.
