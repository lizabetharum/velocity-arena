4-Week Velocity Arena Roadmap

One-page overview of the 20-day arc. Each week shows the core math, what students produce, what you must do, and what to watch for.

Week 1
Stats, formulas, and bot behavior
  • Core math
    Proportional relationships, percent, rate of change, and basic coordinate ideas through Speed, Endurance, Turning, and Power formulas.
  • Student work
    Stat cards with a 20-point allocation, formula worksheets (Speed, Endurance, Power), early team dashboards, and written one-sentence explanations using sentence frames.
  • You prepare
    Printed stat cards, Day 1 Pre-Task Diagnostic, Speed/Endurance/Power worksheets, visible "Math Vocabulary Wall" space, and an Operations Quick Reference at each station.
  • Routines to establish
    Daily launch → formula on board with one worked example → 30-40 minutes of team Challenge Block work with visible time checkpoints → 5-10 minute share-out and ENDS prompt.
  • Watch for
    Campers stuck on operations (division, squaring, order of operations), ELLs leaving writing prompts blank, and advanced campers finishing calculations 10-15 minutes early.
  • Key moves
    Quietly point to Operations Quick Reference (no reteach mini-lesson), add sentence frames to written prompts, and give early finishers extension cards tied to the same formulas.
Week 2
Graphs, distance, and match strategy
  • Core math
    Scatter plots, linear relationships between stats and goals, and distance formula on the 8×4 field grid.
  • Student work
    Class Speed vs. Goals scatter plot, card-sorts matching situations, tables, graphs, and equations, and distance-formula calculations from tip-off and actual match coordinates.
  • You prepare
    Printed graph sets (verbal / table / graph / equation), large field grid on the floor or board, distance-formula worksheets, and updated dashboard access for match data.
  • Routines to reinforce
    "Subtract, square, add, square root" chant for distance formula, grid-check verification (students count diagonals to check their d), and structured share-outs that ask "What does this slope/distance mean in bot terms?".
  • Watch for
    Students swapping x and y, mis-reading decimals, or treating every relationship as proportional. Highlight teams whose actual goals do not match a proportional prediction.
  • Key moves
    Use sentence frames for "line of best fit" interpretations, assign Model Tracker or Checker roles to advanced campers, and prompt ELLs to use visuals and the vocabulary wall when explaining graphs.
Week 3
Inequalities, linear models, and proposals
  • Core math
    Systems of inequalities for valid stat loadouts, building and testing linear equations (y = mx + b) from real dashboard data, and percent change across seasons.
  • Student work
    Tables of valid loadouts under multiple constraints, handwritten equations with labeled slope and intercept from two matches, residual tables comparing predicted and actual win probabilities, and draft commissioner proposals.
  • You prepare
    Constraint tables, proposal worksheets with required data points, percent-change worksheets using Season 1 vs. Season 2 stats, and clear norms for match-day data logging.
  • Routines to reinforce
    "Build equation from two real points → test on three new points → read residuals aloud," and using the dashboard as the single source of truth for any number cited in a proposal.
  • Watch for
    Students switching x and y in slope, treating a single outlier as proof, or proposing rules that mainly benefit their own team instead of the last-place team.
  • Key moves
    Use the Verification worksheet to force "claim → dashboard location → verified number," have one camper per team ready to explain a calculation from memory, and assign a Math Verifier role during the Commissioners Meeting.
Week 4
Refinement, explanation, and exhibition
  • Core math
    Revisiting percent change, re-allocating stats based on evidence, and turning data and formulas into clear explanations for visitors and the final diagnostic.
  • Student work
    Finalized stat configurations, revised proposals after conflict-of-interest checks, ENDS journal reflections making sense of strategy, and visitor teaching tasks at exhibition.
  • You prepare
    Post-Task Diagnostic access, exhibition stations with simple visual explanation cards, and a clear checklist for what each team must have ready (bot, dashboard, one explanation card, one spokesperson).
  • Routines to reinforce
    "Data first, story second" norm: any claim about improvement must point to a specific dashboard number or worksheet calculation; one camper per team can explain a key formula aloud.
  • Watch for
    Teams revising stories without revising numbers, or ELL campers whose math is strong but verbal explanation is blocked; use visual explanation cards and sentence frames to support them.
  • Key moves
    Allow journals in a first language with a short English summary, prepare one visual explanation per ELL camper, and keep exhibition assessment focused on whether visitors can complete the task independently.
