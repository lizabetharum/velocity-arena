Formula
% Change = ((New − Old) ÷ Old) × 100
Work through board example before teams start.
Key teaching: Is percent change proportional?
Speed: +50% stat → +50% motor power (proportional ✓)
Power: +50% Power → +125% charge speed (NOT proportional ✗)

Zero-value warning: If S1 stat = 0, percent change is undefined — cannot divide by zero. Use absolute change instead and note it on the worksheet.
Students Produce

  • Biggest-change stat named with direction
  • Written answer: is their biggest change proportional? Why?

Say Script
Launch The auction just locked your Season 2 configuration. Percent change is the tool that turns that decision into data. You can now calculate exactly how much each of your four stats shifted from Season 1 to Season 2.
Formula Intro Percent change equals new minus old, divided by old, times 100. That's it. Let's do one together on the board.
Zero Value If your Season 1 value is zero, you cannot calculate percent change — you'd be dividing by zero. Write "undefined" and calculate the absolute change instead: new minus old, with no division.
Proportionality Q Here is a question for the whole room: if a team increased Speed by 50%, should their wins increase by 50%? Does it depend on which stat changed? Then: The answer depends on whether the relationship is proportional. Speed is — Power is not. That is why we calculate both.
Prediction Link Look at your Prediction Market card. If you raised Speed by 50% and your prediction calls for more wins — can you explain the link? If you can, your prediction is evidence-based. If you can't, it's still a guess.
