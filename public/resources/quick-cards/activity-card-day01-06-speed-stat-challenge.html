Speed Stat Challenge
6 of 9
Day 1 · Challenge Block · 40 min
Follows Pre-Task Diagnostic
MATH: Motor Power = (Speed × 100) ÷ 20  ·  Each stat point = 5% motor power  ·  Speed × 5 = Power %
Before You Start
  • Formula written on board before anyone sits down
  • Speed Challenge worksheets printed — 1 per team
  • Stat cards at every seat, face up
  • Comparison team pairings decided and written down
  • Timer set to 40 min — you'll call 0:05, 0:20, 0:30, 0:37
  • Your answer key in hand — not projected
  • Do not confirm or deny any answers during team calculation or peer comparison. Redirect to the work.

Students Produce
  • Completed Speed Stat Challenge worksheet — motor power calculations for each Speed value
  • Speed value written on stat card (binding from the moment it is written)
  • Class claim sentence: doubling Speed does / does not double motor power — written on board

Timing
0:00 Formula on board One sentence only — point to the board and step back. Hard cap at 5 minutes. If you're still talking at 0:05, stop mid-sentence: "Worksheet time starts now."
0:05 Team calculation Circulate slowly. Scan for teams that haven't started and values that are clearly wrong. Do not confirm or deny anything. Redirect to the formula.
0:20 Peer comparison Announce pairings, then move to the edge of the room. Teams compare value by value starting at Speed = 1. You do not arbitrate. Silent pairs need a prompt — walk to them.
0:30 Whole-room confirm State all five values. Then ask: "What pattern do you see?" Wait. Do not fill the silence. Accept any correct observation — you don't need formal mathematical language here.
0:37 Stat card entry Teams choose their Speed allocation. Circulate while they decide. Check that they filled in both the points AND the percentage. Stay near teams that look uncertain.
0:40 Hard stop Every team must have a Speed value on their stat card before leaving. Stay at the table until they write it. The entry is binding from the moment it's written.
Say This
Launch
"One formula. Motor power equals Speed points times 100 divided by 20. Your worksheet has five Speed values: 1, 3, 5, 6, and 8. Go."
Compare
"Stop. Turn to your comparison team. Compare your Speed = 1 answer. If you match, move to Speed = 3. Go through all five."
Pattern
"Look at your table. What pattern do you see?"  [wait — do not answer it]
Allocate
"You have seen the formula and calculated five values. Now choose your Speed allocation and write it on your stat card."
Not this
Don't say "linear relationship" — save for Day 4. Don't say "good job" or "perfect" when confirming values — confirm and move on.
Watch For → Redirect
Teams disagree — can't find the split "Write your work for Speed = [X] side by side. Find the step where they diverge." Walk away. Return in 2 min.
Team writes decimals (0.05 not 5%) "What does the ×100 do to that number?" Walk away without staying for the answer.
Team inverted the fraction (2000% for Spd = 1) "For Speed = 1 your answer is 2000%. Can a motor run at 2000%?" That's enough. Don't show them the error.
Team not working — chatting, cards out "Speed = 1. What does the formula give you?" Point to worksheet. One prompt only — walk away.
Both teams agree on a wrong answer "You both got [X] for Speed = 1. Walk me through the first step of the calculation." Don't point to the error.
Team finishes all 5 in < 4 min Extension: "Calculate Speed = 2, 10, and 20. What does Speed = 20 tell you about the formula?"
Hard Stop Every team must write a Speed value on their stat card before leaving the room. Stay at the table until they do. The stat card entry is binding from the moment it's written — do not soften this.
