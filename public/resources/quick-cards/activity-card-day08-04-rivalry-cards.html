Before Block Opens
  • One index card per team at each table — placed before matches end
  • 2+ thick markers per table (cards must be readable from standing distance)
  • Dashboard or wall stat sheet visible from every seat
  • Wall space near leaderboard cleared for posting. Tape or sticky tack ready.
  • Leaderboard updated with Day 8 results. Teams stay seated through the launch script.
Purpose

Students turn a rival's stat allocation into a verifiable claim — not generic trash talk. One rule: every card cites a specific dashboard number. No number = card comes back. Day 9 checks every card publicly.

Students Produce
  • One rivalry card with: rival team name, one specific dashboard number, one verifiable claim
  • Card posted on wall — stays through Day 20 gallery walk

Sample Cards

✔"Speed = 4 means motor runs at 20% power. Ours: 35%. Round 2 isn't close."

Launch Script
Say "Rivalry Cards. One card per team, one rival per card. 20 minutes."
Say "One rule: cite a dashboard number. Not 'you're slow' — 'Speed = 4 = 20% motor power; ours is 35%.' No number = card comes back."
Say "Pick your rival. Write their team name at the top. 30 seconds."
Watch For
Writing without opening the dashboard. Stop them before they write a full card. "Write what the dashboard says — not what you remember."
Generic trash talk: "You're going down." Send it back: "What number from their dashboard are you basing that on?"
Vague consequence: "Your Endurance is low." "Low means what? What does their bot do at minute 2?" They know the formula. Make them use it.
Personal attack: "You suck at driving." Send it back: stats only — not the driver.
