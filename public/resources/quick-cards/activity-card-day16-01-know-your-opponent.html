Know Your Opponent
1 of 4
Day 16 · Session 01
20 min · Hook
📊 Stat card flip → physical vote (stand for your biggest threat) → bar chart S/E/T/P (shape, not exact) → circle threat stat → ball mode assigned
Before Students Arrive
  • Pull each team's opponent stat card off the wall
  • Place face-down on that team's table — one card per table
  • Do not explain. Let them see it when they walk in.
Ball Mode Key
  • Circled Speed → Chaos ball this afternoon
  • Circled Power → Heavy Touch ball
  • Circled Endurance → two-ball match
Produces
  • Index card bar chart posted at every table
  • One circled threat stat per team
  • Ball mode assignments for afternoon
Alt: Human Radar Chart
For high-energy or flat rooms: tape 4 room corners (Speed, Endurance, Turning, Power). Teams physically walk to their threat stat. Count clusters, name them, return to draw.
Sequence
0:00The Flip"That card is your Championship opponent. Flip it. 90 seconds — look at the numbers, talk to your team, don't write yet." Start timer. Let room be loud. That noise is correct.
0:02Stat reminderSay once, under 20 sec: "Speed = how fast. Endurance = how long before slowing. Turning = redirect sharpness. Power = how hard it hits." Move on.
0:02The Physical VoteCall each stat: "If this stat is your biggest threat — stand up. Whole team stands or nobody stands." Speed → Endurance → Turning → Power. Pause 5 sec each. Name what you see out loud after each.
0:10Tally + bridge"Which stat had the most teams standing?" Take the answer. "Remember that — it determines your ball mode this afternoon."
0:10The Bar ChartHand out index card + marker. "4 bars: S, E, T, P. Biggest number = tallest bar. Smallest = shortest. Opponent name at top. One word at bottom: their biggest threat stat. 8 minutes." That's the whole instruction.
0:1730-sec warning"Thirty seconds. Circle the bar you voted for."
0:18CloseEach team holds up card, reads their one word. Write on board next to team name. 6 words. Done. Deliver ball-mode consequence.
Say
Flip"That card on your table is your Championship opponent. Flip it over. You have 90 seconds. Look at the numbers. Talk to your team. Don't write anything yet."
Vote"If that stat is your biggest threat — meaning if it's high on their card, your chances of winning go down — stand up. Not the stat you like. The one that could beat you."
Bar chart"If they ask 'how tall exactly?' — say: tallest bar for their biggest number, shortest for their smallest. That's all."
Close"This morning you put numbers behind what you just drew. The bar you circled is where you start. That same stat is your target this afternoon — and it just told me what kind of game you're afraid of."
Watch For
Team won't stand for any stat — walk over quietly: "Which number is biggest on that card?" That's their answer. No opting out.
Two teams scout each other, both stand for same stat — point it out: "You're both worried about each other's Speed. That match is going to be interesting."
Bar chart shape doesn't match actual numbers — point at the number, point at the bar. No words needed.
Week 4 flat energy — run Human Radar Chart variant (see Col 1) instead of stand-at-desks vote
Hard Stop0:20 — index cards posted at every table, 6 threat stats on board, ball modes assigned for afternoon
