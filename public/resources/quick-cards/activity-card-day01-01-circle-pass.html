Circle Pass
1 of 9
Day 1 · Hook Block · 20 min
First activity of the day
PURPOSE: Build collaborative attention and establish that the room is a safe place to be imperfect — before any math begins.
Before You Start
  • Clear floor space for full group to stand in a circle
  • No chairs inside the circle — remove before students arrive
  • Arrive early and shape the space yourself
  • Do not ask students to move furniture mid-activity.
  • Have your demo sound and movement ready — make it slightly awkward on purpose.
Students Produce
  • No written artifact
  • Each student shares name + one claim about what robots can do — verbal only
Timing
0:00Opening + demoGet everyone standing and placed in a circle — point, don't ask. Say the instructions once, then demonstrate before anyone can overthink it. Keep your demo slightly awkward.
0:02Round 1Start the pass and step back. Don't narrate or coach. Let it get messy. Watch who freezes, who laughs, who helps neighbors unprompted.
0:07First ReverseCall "Reverse" before the sound completes the full loop — the disruption is intentional. Say nothing else. Let them figure it out.
0:08Round 2Students now escalate — bigger sounds, stranger movements. Let them. Call a second "Reverse" midway. Watch if the group adapts faster than the first time.
0:13Round 3Let this run until you feel the energy peak. Someone makes a genuinely absurd sound. That is your cue to stop.
0:18CloseSay the close exactly. Do not explain further. Let it land, then move directly to Mirror Pairs.
Say This
Launch
"I make a sound and a movement — any sound, any movement. The person to my left copies exactly, then immediately adds their own. There is no correct version of this. If you blank, copy the person before you and change one small thing."
Reverse
"Reverse."
Close
"Notice something: none of you needed instructions to figure out what to do. You watched, you adapted, you built on what came before you. That's the skill you'll use every day in this program."
Not this
Don't narrate while the pass is moving. Don't coach volume or energy. Don't rescue freezing students too fast — 5-second wait first.
Watch For → Redirect
Group freezesWait 5 seconds. Then: "Copy the person before you and add one thing. Doesn't matter what." Don't rescue too fast.
Group goes too quietDon't tell them to be louder. Join the circle yourself and turn up your own volume.
Group goes too chaoticCall "Reverse." The direction change resets energy without calling anyone out.
One student refuses to participateDon't pressure. Let them observe. They may join a later round. This conversation happens after, not during.
