MATH: Finding must contain a number, formula, or named variable. Vote criterion: most mathematically interesting. LO 9.1
Before Session
  • Index cards (4×6″ min) and markers at every table — one card per team, large enough to read from 2 feet
  • Dot stickers at every table — at least 3 stickers per student
  • Clear wall space with enough spread that students walk between cards
  • Post vote criterion before 9:00 and leave up for the full 20 min: "Most mathematically interesting finding — who learned the most from one match result."
Card Format (Three Required Elements)
  1. Stat configuration — S=6, P=3, T=5, E=6
  2. Match result — Goals for · Goals against · W/L
  3. Finding (one sentence) — must contain a number, formula, or named variable. Not "we played well."

Example: "Our scatter plot equation predicted 3 goals; we scored 4. Speed at 6 was the variable that closed the gap."

Students Produce
  • One index card posted on wall
  • Dot votes on 3 other teams' cards (criterion: most mathematically interesting)

Launch Script
Say "Season 1 is done. On your index card, write three things: your stat configuration for your best match, the result of that match, and one sentence about what you found. The sentence has to have a number in it. 'Best' does not mean biggest win — it means the match where your math was most useful. A loss can be your best match if your model told you something true. 4 minutes. Post the card when done."
Watch For
Team chooses biggest win regardless of what data shows. "Did your equation predict that result, or did it surprise you?" If it surprised them, that match is more interesting. Redirect without overriding.
Team with zero wins says they have no best match. "Which of your matches had the smallest gap between prediction and actual? Write that one." A 0-win team with good predictions has a valid best match.
Finding sentence has no number. "What number from your data log are you referring to?" Do not let them post without one.
Finding is too long (runs to a second line). One sentence. If it runs onto a second line, it is too long. Cut it.
Struggling to pick a match. "Which match had the smallest gap between your prediction and actual?" That is usually their best match by this criterion.
Bridge to Distance Formula

The cards prime the next block: students are about to calculate straight-line distances between coordinates from those same matches. Name it: "The card on the wall is the qualitative version of data you will compute with in the next 50 minutes."
