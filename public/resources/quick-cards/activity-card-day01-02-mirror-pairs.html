Mirror Pairs
2 of 9
Day 1 · Hook Block · 15 min
Follows Circle Pass
PURPOSE: Build non-verbal attention and team calibration — the same awareness students need to read a partner's code and adapt under pressure.
Before You Start
  • Clear floor space for pairs to face each other with room to move
  • Standing pairs — not seated at desks
  • Assign pairs yourself before the activity starts — do not let students self-select
  • Check for existing conflicts in your pair assignments and split them now
  • Mixed comfort levels work better than friend groups for this activity.
Students Produce
  • No written artifact
  • Verbal agreement statement: what both partners' robots will have in common
Timing
0:00OpeningGive instructions once, then clarify the one thing people always ask: "The leader doesn't speed up to test their partner. Move at the pace of someone who wants to be understood."
0:02Round 1 — assigned leaderAssign the first leader with a neutral rule (birthday soonest / person on the left). Set timer. Go silent. Walk the room slowly — don't coach pairs unless completely stuck.
0:04Swap"Swap. The mirror becomes the leader." That's all. Let them re-find the rhythm.
0:04:30Round 2Same conditions, same silence. Watch for the pair that locks in — that's the one to reference in the close.
0:07Round 3 — no leaderSet the timer. Walk the room. Say nothing. The silence is the instruction. Don't fill it.
0:10CloseAsk the question, wait for hands, connect to the program. Then move on — don't extend discussion.
Say This
Launch
"Face your partner. One of you leads. One of you mirrors. No talking. No judging. No winner. Move slowly enough that your partner can stay with you."
Round 3
"This time, neither of you leads. Move together. No one is in charge. If you have to talk about how that's possible, you'll miss it."
Close
"How many of you knew within the first 10 seconds who was actually leading in that last round? That's attention. When two people are paying attention to each other, patterns emerge without being named."
Not this
Don't force eye contact — let pairs focus on hands or body movement. Don't intervene when one partner takes over in Round 3; the pair should notice it themselves.
Watch For → Redirect
One partner keeps taking over in Round 3Don't intervene. Let the pair notice it themselves. It's useful data for them and worth naming in the close.
Pair collapses into laughterGive them a beat, then say quietly: "Whenever you're ready." They'll come back. Don't call them out.
Pair won't make eye contactDon't force it. Let them focus on hands or general body movement. The activity still works.
Conflict pair identified mid-activityYou should have caught this in prep. If not — let them finish the round, then quietly reassign for Round 3.
