Mirror — Pairs
2 of 8
Day 1 · Hook-Improv/Trust · 15 min
Follows Circle Pass
MATH:
Before You Start
  • Clear floor space for pairs to face each other
  • Assign pairs yourself (mixed comfort levels, not friend groups)
  • No materials needed
  • Think about a group situation where no one was paying attention to each other. What broke down? What would have fixed it?
Students Produce
  • Synchronized mirrored movement in Round 1
  • Synchronized movement with role swap in Round 2
  • Shared leadership movement with no designated leader in Round 3
Reference
Round 3 is the pedagogical core. Protect the debrief.
Timing
0:00–0:02OpeningFrame activity, assign pairs, deliver instructions.
0:02–0:04Round 1Designated leader mirrors. Facilitator silent.
0:04–0:05SwapMirror becomes leader.
0:05–0:07Round 2New leader. Facilitator monitors silently.
0:07–0:10Round 3No designated leader. Move together without talking.
0:10–0:13DebriefThree-move discussion: gathering, probing, revoicing.
Say This
Open
Think about the last time you were actually in sync with someone. Playing a sport, finishing each other's sentences, moving through a space together without bumping. What made that possible?
Instructions
Face your partner. One leads, one mirrors. Leader moves slowly and deliberately. Mirror copies exactly, in real time. No talking. No judging. No winner. Two people paying attention to each other.
Hard Stop
The leader doesn't speed up to test their partner. Move at the pace of someone who wants to be understood.
Round 3
Neither of you leads. Move together. No one is in charge. If you have to talk about how that's possible, you'll miss it.
Not This
Filling the silence during rounds. The silence is the point.
Watch For → Redirect
Pairs slow down and lock in immediatelyNote them — call on them in the debrief as models.
One person keeps speeding up or testing the mirrorMove close, say quietly: 'Move at a pace you'd want back.'
Pair in sustained laughter, can't recoverGive them a beat, then quietly: 'Whenever you're ready.'
Pair genuinely stuck and frustrated in Round 3Say quietly: 'Just start moving and let your partner find you.'
Round 3 never clicks — pairs stay in leader/mirror modeDon't extend. Move to debrief early. Rephrase question: 'What would it have taken?'
One partner keeps taking over in Round 3Don't intervene. Let pair notice. Useful data emerges in debrief.
Hard Stop The leader doesn't speed up to test their partner. Move at the pace of someone who wants to be understood.
