MATH: Endurance experimentation — try different E values, observe decay behavior. Closing Ritual: name one math concept, write the full calculation.
Before You Start
  • Bots powered and accessible — no reconnecting needed
  • MakeCode open on all devices with Endurance decay code still loaded
  • Closing Ritual prompt visible on board or as handout
  • Room is coming off a social, creative block. Do not re-launch technically. One sentence is enough. The nudge toward Endurance experimentation belongs in that sentence — but lightly.
Students Produce
  • Closing Ritual: one math concept from today written with the full calculation
  • Explanation shared verbally with a teammate: what the concept means about the bot's behavior
Closing Ritual Prompt (on board)
"Name one math concept you used today. Write the full calculation. Share it with a teammate and explain what it means about your bot — not in formula terms, but in what-your-bot-actually-does terms."
The One Question Rule

"What are you trying?"

Not "What are you doing?" — that implies evaluation. After they answer, say "That's interesting" and move on, or ask one follow-up at most. Then leave the table.

Dead space is not failure. A team that talks for 5 minutes before touching the code is using the time.

Say This
Launch
"Try a different Endurance value in the code. See what happens."
Stat card Q
"The card is fixed. The code is for observation. You're not changing your allocation — you're seeing what a different number would do."
Stuck team
"What do you want to try?" Then wait. Do not fill the silence. The first thing they say after 5 seconds is theirs — not yours.
Not this
Don't suggest specific values: "What would happen if you tried E=15?" is a direction disguised as a question. Don't answer "What should we…" — redirect: "What do you want to do?"
Watch For → Redirect
Room doesn't engage after launch sentenceWalk to the nearest team and ask "What do you want to try?" loud enough for adjacent teams to hear. One question restarts the room.
Team re-runs exact same test — no changesValid choice. Don't redirect. "Getting the same result?" If yes, let them be. Confirmation is self-directed activity.
Team experiments with Turning or Power values"Endurance is today's stat. Turning is tomorrow. Come back to E for now." If they push back, let it go — early curiosity about Day 3 content is a feature.
