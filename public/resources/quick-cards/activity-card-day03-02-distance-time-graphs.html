MATH: d = √((x₂–x₁)² + (y₂–y₁)²)  ·  UNIT = 27 cm/square  ·  Endurance decay visible in graph slope
Before You Start
  • Data cards printed — alternate Card A / Card B between adjacent teams. Sheet 1: data + blank graph. Sheet 2: answer (hold until 14:00).
  • Floor grid taped: 8×4, origin (0,0) labeled. UNIT = 27 cm per square.
  • Distance formula on board — leave it up all session
  • Laptops open to MakeCode project from Day 2
Students Produce
  • Plotted distance-time graph on Card A or B worksheet (both runs on one graph, labeled)
  • Written explanation sentence: why the two lines look different
  • moveToCoordinate function coded in MakeCode with UNIT = 27
  • At least one navigation run logged in the data dashboard

Timing — 3 Parts
0:00Transition"You just spent 25 min being a robot. Your bot does the same — executes exactly what the code says. Today: look at what it actually did, figure out why, write better instructions."
1:00Part 1: Graphs — 16 minDistribute Card A/B alternating. "Two runs on one graph. Label each line. One sentence: why do they look different? 15 minutes. Work with your team." Circulate without correcting.
12:00Peer share"Stop. Read your sentence to the team next to you." 2 min. Then distribute Sheet 2. "Compare their stat allocations to your explanation. 90 seconds."
15:30Pull together"The flat line = high Endurance. The steep drop = low Endurance. The bot slowed down because the formula said so." 30 sec for A/B teams to compare graphs — they look opposite but show the same relationship. Hard stop.
17:00Part 2: Walk the PlaneGrid walk. Phase 1 (5 min): call coordinates, students move. Phase 2 (10 min): "Walk (1,1) to (4,5). Estimate first — don't count steps." Write formula on board. Calculate. (= 5.) Run (2,1)→(6,4) and (0,0)→(3,4). Both = 5. "You'll use this with match data on Day 10."
32:00Part 3: Code — 30 minUNIT = 27. moveToCoordinate(x, y): check sign → turn direction → forward distance × UNIT. Button A: go to (3,2). Button B: (3,2)→(5,4). A+B: full route. Dashboard row every run.
62:00Hard stopmoveToCoordinate must be working before lunch. Lunch follows this block.
Say This
Graphs
"Flat line = high Endurance. Steep drop = low Endurance. The bot slowed down because the formula said so — not because it was programmed badly."
Formula
"x₁ and y₁ are where you started. x₂ and y₂ are where you ended. You were just standing at both of those points."
Code
"UNIT equals 27. That's how wide each grid square is in centimeters. The function translates a coordinate into a distance and drives there."
Not this
Don't flag that all three formula answers = 5. Don't correct graph errors during work time — let the peer share or Sheet 2 surface them. Don't skip the dashboard row.
Watch For → Redirect
Weak explanation: "Run B used more power""What specifically caused the drop? Point to a number." Push for Endurance before flipping to Sheet 2.
Team plots incorrectly — wrong axis or pointsDon't correct during work time. Let the peer share or Sheet 2 reveal it. The error is the debrief moment.
Estimate wildly off from calculated distance"Don't correct your estimate — write both numbers and compare." Estimation error is data.
moveToCoordinate not running by 60 minCheck: UNIT set? Function called in a button block? Get one test run before lunch — it's the foundation for the Arc block this afternoon.
