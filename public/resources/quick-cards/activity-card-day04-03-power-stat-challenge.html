Follows Power Allocation
MATH: Charge Speed = Power² · P=4 → 16% · P=8 → 64% · Doubling Power quadruples speed · Physical ratio ≈ 1:2.5–1:3 (not 1:4)
Before You Start
  • Tape 1 on floor: bot nose position, 40 cm from ball
  • Tape 2 on floor: ball start position (separate mark)
  • MakeCode template open on every device: Button A → forward [16]% → pause 3500ms → stop
  • Ruler or tape measure at each station
  • Run one demo yourself; record distance as your reference
  • If any bot stalls at 16%, swap it before students arrive and note the team
Students Produce
  • Power table: P = 1, 2, 4, 8 → charge speed %
  • Gap sentence: "The ratio predicted ___ cm. The bot did ___ cm. The gap is ___ cm."
  • Power stat recorded on stat card

Timing
0–5Intro "Charge Speed = Power²." Write it. "Fill in the table. 10 minutes. Go." Step back.
5–15Table Work Walk quietly. Watch for multiplying instead of squaring (P4 → 8% not 16%). Redirect: "What does the exponent mean in Power²?" Teams done early: calculate P3.
15–20Ratios "4% to 16%?" → 1:4. "16% to 64%?" → 1:4. "Double Power = 4× output. You're about to test that physically."
20–30Test: P4 Set to 16%. Bot nose on Tape 1, ball on Tape 2. Press A. Measure ball travel. Code interaction limit: 90 sec.
30–38Test: P8 Set to 64%. Full reset: bot to Tape 1, ball to Tape 2. Non-negotiable — different start = bad comparison. Run. Record.
38–43Data Log Predicted P8 = 4 × P4 result. Actual ≈ 100–130 cm. One gap sentence — the sentence forces precision.
43–48Key Move "Is your gap a problem — or is it information?" Wait. Do not fill the silence. First good answer names one thing the ratio didn't account for.
48–50Hard Stop Power stat recorded on every stat card before the table breaks up.
Say This
Intro
"One rule. Charge motor speed equals Power points squared. Write that rule. Fill in the table. Go."
After ratios
"Every time you double the points, the output multiplies by four. That is the rule you are about to test physically."
Key move
"Look at your gap. Is it a problem — or is it information?"
Close
"Power is the only stat that squares. That gap is physics the formula doesn't model. Your job is to use the formula and understand where it breaks."
Not this
Don't tell them the gap is fine. Ask whether it's a problem or information. Let them decide.
Watch For → Redirect
Squaring error: P4 → 8% "What does the exponent mean in Power²?" Don't give the answer.
No reset between runs Non-negotiable. Different start position invalidates the comparison entirely.
"The gap means we're wrong" "Did you follow the setup exactly? Then the gap isn't your error — what might it be from?"
P8 result > 5× P4 Setup changed. Reset tape marks. Re-run once only. Document what changed.
Hard Stop Power stat recorded on every stat card before the table breaks up.
