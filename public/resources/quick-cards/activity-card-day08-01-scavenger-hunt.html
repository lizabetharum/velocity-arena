Before the Session
  • Build card chain using real Day 7 leaderboard data — do this before Day 8 morning, not the same day
  • Post 6–8 problem cards around the room (walls, windows, chairs) — spread far enough that students are physically moving
  • Place 6–8 answer cards face-down at one central table, sorted lowest to highest by answer value
  • Print one answer sheet per student (half-sheet) — they write their calculation for each card
  • Verify chain loops: trace it yourself once from Card 1 back to Card 1 before the session
  • Card chain template: docs.google.com/document/d/1EQUtQ2J-FJTI…
How the Chain Works

Each problem card shows a real team's stat from Day 7. The back has one calculation. Student works it, finds the matching answer card at the center table. The back of that answer card directs them to the next problem card. Chain loops back to Card 1.

Self-correcting: a wrong answer is not in the pile. Student debugs without you confirming anything.

Students Produce
  • Answer sheet with calculation shown for each card visited
  • Self-corrected work (wrong answer forces recalculation)

What Counts as a Finding

Name a mathematical pattern: a common error on one formula type, two teams with the same stat producing different outputs, or a calculation that surprised students. Not who completed the most cards.

Launch Script
Say "Yesterday's leaderboard is on the wall. Today it's on the cards around the room. Each card shows a real team's stat from yesterday. The back has one calculation. Work it out on your answer sheet, then come to the center table and find your answer in the pile. When you find it, the back of that answer card tells you where to go next. The chain loops. Go as far as you can in 15 minutes."
Say "One rule: if your answer isn't in the pile, you got it wrong. Go back and recalculate. Do not ask me if your answer is right. The pile tells you."
Watch For
Student copies from someone else's sheet. Before they pick up the answer card: "Walk me through that calculation." If they can't, send them back to the problem card. Handle quietly — don't announce to the room.
Answer card missing from the pile. Check the floor. Keep a spare copy of every answer card behind the center table for this exact situation.
Student stuck on same card 3+ min. "Which formula applies here?" Not the answer — the formula. Then walk away.
Student asks "Is this right?" "If your answer is in the pile, it's right." Do not confirm anything else.
