Before Session
  • Timer visible to room
  • Run order list on board (blank — teams fill during commitment)
  • Observation Log worksheets at every seat
  • Stat cards on desks
  • Clear sight lines from all seats to running area
Time Math
~3 min per team. 6 teams = 18 min. 8 teams = 24 min. 2-min Commitment and 10-min Adjustment Window are fixed regardless of team count.
Produces
  • Observation Logs: one math-connected observation per team watched
  • Season 1 launch sequence locked per team
  • Adjustment written on log before any changes made
Lock Rule
ONE change in the adjustment window — not two, not a rebuild. If a team argues "two changes are really one," the rule stands. One exception creates precedent.

Say
Commitment"The sequence you write is the sequence you run. Once the showcase starts, it is locked. No exceptions."
Observation standard"'They went fast' is a description. 'Their bot reached the ball in under 3 seconds, which means their Speed allocation is probably above 8' — that is an observation. You connect what you saw to a stat or formula."
Adjustment"You may make one adjustment. Not two. Not a full rebuild. One change. Write it in the Final Adjustment box before you touch anything."
Scouting connect"You are watching like scouts now. Those observations are yours — you will use them again on Day 15 when you write your scouting reports."
Watch For
Team makes 2+ changes in adjustment window — "Name the single variable that changed. If you can't, it's more than one change."
Team modifies bot after Season 1 lock — season integrity issue — revert immediately, note in log
Observation with no math connection — "Good start. What number would you need to see to make that inference?"
