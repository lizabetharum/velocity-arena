Slow Down, Slow Down
2 of 8
Day 2 · Hook Block · 15 min
Follows Failure Bow
MATH: P(t) = 100 – (20–E) × 1.5 × t  ·  Students' bodies model the formula before they calculate it.
Before You Start
  • Clear floor space — everyone stands and moves
  • No materials needed
  • Know your pace: start at 10 reps/round, subtract 1 per round
  • Do the activity with them. You model the deceleration — don't just call it. The physical experience is the point.
Students Produce
  • No written artifact
  • Verbal naming of Endurance as the concept that describes deceleration
Formula Bridge
P(t) = 100 – (20–E) × 1.5 × t
Students' bodies just ran this formula. Their rep rate was P(t). Each round was one time step. Their Endurance was fixed — and low.
The point: a higher Endurance value means a slower decay rate. A lower Endurance means what just happened to their jumping jacks.
Don't explain this before the activity. Let them feel it first.
Timing
0:00Launch"Stand up. Jumping jacks. Full speed. Follow my count." Do them with the room. 10 reps together.
0:30Slow it down"Slower." 9 reps. Then 8. Call each new round. Keep subtracting. Don't narrate — just count and slow.
4:00Near stopRoom is at 1–2 reps per round, or stopped. Hold the slow pace for one full round so they feel the end state.
5:00Sit down"Sit. What just happened to you?" Wait 10 seconds. Let someone answer before you do.
7:00Name it"What would we call it if your bot did exactly what you just did?" Endurance. Write the word on the board.
10:00Formula preview"There's a formula that describes what your body just did. You'll build it in the next block." Write P(t) = 100 – (20–E) × 1.5 × t on the board. Don't explain it yet.
13:00Bridge"Your Endurance number controls how fast your bot slows down. Low Endurance = what just happened to you. High Endurance = barely any change."
Say This
Launch
"Stand up. We're doing jumping jacks. Full speed. Go."
After stop
"What happened between the first set and the last set?" [pause] "Your rate changed. You started fast and ended slow. That has a name."
Formula
"P of t equals 100 minus a decay rate times time. Your body ran that formula just now. t was the round number. P was how many reps you could do."
Not this
Don't explain the formula before the activity. Don't skip students who can't do jumping jacks — have them pump arms while seated. Don't let the debrief run past 13:00.
Watch For → Redirect
Student can't do jumping jacks physically"Stand and pump your arms instead." Or pump arms while seated. Don't skip them — they need the somatic experience.
Students stop before the pace gets low"Keep going. All the way down." The near-zero moment is the learning — they need to feel the end state.
Room jumps straight to formula talkLet one comment land. Then redirect: "Hold the math — what did your body do first?" Re-ground in the physical before the abstract.
Students ask "What's our Endurance number?""That's next. First let's see what the formula looks like." Allocation is the block after this one.
