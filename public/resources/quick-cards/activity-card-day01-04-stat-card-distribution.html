Stat Card Distribution
4 of 9
Day 1 · Hook Block · 5 min
Follows Driving Question Reveal
PURPOSE: Introduce the stat card as a four-day commitment structure. One stat at a time. The blank rows communicate four decisions ahead, each earned — not given.
Before You Start
  • Print one stat card per student — 4 row labels and formulas pre-printed
  • All four allocation fields are blank
  • Three rows (Endurance, Turning, Power) are visually greyed out — only Speed is active
  • Do not let students see the card before you say the one-sentence framing.
  • The card is binding from the moment each stat is entered. Do not soften this.
Students Produce
  • Stat card received — student name written on card
  • Speed value identified and discussed (not yet written — that happens in the Speed Challenge)
If They Ask…
"What are the other rows for?""You'll find out. One line at a time."
"Can I see the formulas now?""They're on the card. You'll use them when each stat comes up."
"What if I mess up?""Once it's written, it stands. Write carefully."
"How many points total?""Twenty. Across all four rows. You'll see."
"Can we work as a team?""Each person fills in their own card. Team name goes top right."
Timing
0:00Hand out cardsHand each student a card face up in landscape orientation. No preamble. No explanation before the card is in their hands.
0:01One sentence"This is your stat card. You will fill it in over the next four days. Today you fill in one line." Do not elaborate. That is the complete introduction.
0:01:30Field questionsAnswer in one sentence and keep moving. Use the Q&A reference at left — do not deviate. Keep moving toward the Speed activity.
0:03Binding rule"You have 20 points across four stats. Today you decide Speed. The card is binding from the moment each stat is entered." Do not soften this.
0:04:30Transition"Cards down. On your feet." Move directly into the Speed Stat Challenge. Do not pause or explain Speed first.
Say This
Distribute
"This is your stat card. You will fill it in over the next four days. Today you fill in one line."
Binding rule
"You have 20 points total across four stats. Today you decide Speed. The card is binding from the moment each stat is entered."
Transition
"Cards down. On your feet."
Not this
Don't explain the whole system on Day 1. Don't say "you can always change it later" — you cannot. Don't elaborate beyond the one sentence unless directly asked.
Watch For → Redirect
Student starts filling in greyed-out rows"Those rows are locked until each stat comes up this week. Today is Speed only."
Student asks to compare cards with teammates"Each person fills in their own card." One sentence. Move on.
Student panics about committing"You don't fill in Speed until after the challenge. You'll have data to decide with." That's all.
Student writes a value immediatelyLet them. The Speed Stat Challenge hasn't started yet — they'll see why data matters. Don't intervene.
Hard Stop Every student must have a stat card in hand before the Speed Stat Challenge begins. Count cards against your roster now.
