Before Block Starts
  • Write on board (leave up all block): d = √((x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²)
  • One Distance Formula worksheet per team
  • Season 1 data log at each table — needs at least 3 match entries with coordinate pairs
  • Grid reference sheet at each table (or physical field grid must be visible)
  • Best Match Gallery Walk cards stay posted — teams may reference when selecting pairs
Math Context (LO 3.2)

Connection to Day 3 Walk the Plane: students physically walked coordinate pairs and counted steps. Today the same distance becomes an algebraic calculation. Every result is checkable against the grid — errors are findable, not invisible.

Students pick 3 coordinate pairs from their actual Season 1 match data, transfer to worksheet, then calculate straight-line distance for each.

Students Produce
  • 3 distance calculations with work shown (formula → substitution → result)
  • Grid check: count diagonal squares to verify each answer
  • One team's first pair shared aloud with class

Intro Script
Say "On Day 3 you walked the coordinate plane. You moved from one point to another, counted steps, and found the straight-line distance with your body. Today you use the formula that does that calculation without counting. Your bot has been driving on the same grid all week. You're going to calculate the actual straight-line distance for three of your Season 1 matches — not how far the bot drove along the field, but the shortcut: the direct line from start to finish."
Grid Anchor "Stand up and point to the two places on the grid where your bot was. Draw a straight line between them on your grid paper. That line is what you're calculating the length of. After you get your number, count the grid squares on the diagonal. Does your calculated number match what you see? On Day 3 you counted steps to check. Today you have a formula. Both should give the same answer."
Watch For — Common Errors
Wrong subtraction order: x₁−x₂ instead of x₂−x₁. "Does the order of subtraction matter when you square the result? Try both and compare." Let the squaring resolve it — don't tell them.
Skips square root — records (x₂−x₁)²+(y₂−y₁)² as distance. "Is that your final answer, or the number inside the square root?" Point to the √ symbol on the board. Redirect to Step 5 on worksheet.
Cannot find coordinate pairs in data log. "Look at your match entries. Where was your bot at the start? Where did it end up? Estimate from the field grid if needed." An estimate with a note is valid.
Early finishers — all three pairs done. "Check each answer against the grid by counting diagonal squares. If your count doesn't match, there's a calculation error somewhere."
Calculated distance far exceeds grid size. Grid is ~20×20 units max. Distance >28 is impossible. Ask them to check their coordinate pairs first — they may have flipped x and y.
Closing Script
Say "The number you calculated is the real straight-line distance your bot would have traveled if it could teleport. Every match, your bot took the scenic route. Your formula found the shortcut. Same grid, same points — one method your body, one method algebra."
Hard Stop10:10 — Transition to Lunch. Grid check required before leaving.
