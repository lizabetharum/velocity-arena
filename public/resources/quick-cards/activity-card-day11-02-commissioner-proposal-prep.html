Formulas
Speed% = (Speed × 100) ÷ 20
Proportional: +50% stat → +50% motor power

Power% = Power²
NOT proportional: +50% Power → +125% charge speed

If Speed = 6 → Speed% = 30%
If Speed = 9 (+50%) → Speed% = 45% (+50% ✓)
If Power = 4 → Power% = 16
If Power = 6 (+50%) → Power% = 36 (+125% ✗)

Say Script
Intro This block has two parts, and they are connected. First, 20 minutes of math: figure out what happens to your bot's behavior when you scale a stat by 50%. Some stats are proportional — when the stat increases by 50%, the output increases by exactly 50%. Some are not. After that, 25 minutes to write your Commissioner proposal. The proposal needs two specific numbers from your dashboard, and it has to name the last-place team. Both parts count.
Bridge to Part 2 The numbers you just calculated are not just math practice. They are a data argument. If you scaled Speed by 50%, your motor power scaled by exactly 50% — proportional. If you scaled Power by 50%, your charge speed went up by 125% — not proportional. If you want to argue that a team should be allowed to scale one stat before Season 2, you now have a formula that tells you exactly what happens. That is evidence. Turn to Part 2.
Misconception 5 min Before you finalize — high Speed correlates with wins doesn't mean Speed caused wins. What else was different in those matches? A proposal that says "Speed wins games" and cites correlation without controlling for other stats will not survive a Commissioner challenge. Add one line: what else was the same in those matches?
Cross-team data OK Citing an opponent's Season 1 record to argue your rule helps the last-place team is valid Commissioner evidence. Confirm: "That data is from the dashboard — cite it."
Hard Stop 10:05 — Transition to Lunch. Every team must have a proposal draft with at least two cited numbers before leaving. No draft = write one sentence with a number before standing up.
