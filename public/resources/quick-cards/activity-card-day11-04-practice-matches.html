Say Script
Opening No official scoring today. Every run you do is a hypothesis test. Before you program anything, you write the hypothesis: what you are changing, what you expect to happen, and why. After the run, you write the result and the conclusion. That is the full entry. You will use these entries in the Open Lab after this. If you run without writing the hypothesis first, the run doesn't count. I will ask every team: What is the hypothesis for this run?
Before Each Run Ask one person to read the hypothesis aloud — just the "if I change X to Y, I expect Z" sentence. If they can't state it: What specifically are you testing? Name the stat and the direction of change. Do not let them start until they can state a specific hypothesis.
Unchanged Config Your proposal argues that [stat] matters. What happens if you test a configuration based on that argument right now? Connect the practice run to the proposal claim.
Wrap 12:55 Count your complete entries. If you have fewer than two, you need at least one more run before Open Lab starts. Open Lab begins in five minutes. Your hypothesis entries are your raw material.
