Say Script
Launch Verification worksheet. 35 minutes. Four sections: verify dashboard numbers, quantify how your rule helps last-place, conflict-of-interest check, name the math concept type. One person per team explains one calculation from memory before lunch. Go.
Number Not on Dashboard Where in the dashboard did this come from? If they can't point to a row: That number comes out. Replace it with one you can find. You have time. A verified smaller number beats an unverified bigger one.
Conflict of Interest Look at Section 3. Run the comparison. If your team gains more than the last-place team — revise the rule so the benefit is more equal, or add a sentence explaining why both gains are fair. Don't go into the meeting with a proposal that reads as self-serving.
Error with Time Left Stay calm: What's the real number? Does it still support your argument? Yes or no. If yes: update and move on. If no: What's the strongest true number you have? Use that one. Do not let them spiral.
Accountability Check To one camper per team (not the team captain): Tell me one calculation from your proposal, from memory. Then tell me what kind of math it is.
Hard Stop 10:00 — Transition to Lunch. Every team must pass all 4 verification checks. No proposal enters the Commissioner's Meeting without a named math concept type in Section 4.
