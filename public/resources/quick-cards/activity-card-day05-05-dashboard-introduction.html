Data Dashboard Introduction
5 of 8
10 Min · Transition Block · Day 5 · PM
Follows Cold Recall
MATH: Residual = Predicted Goals − Actual Score (Week 3 algebra foundation)
Before You Start
  • Open digital dashboard on projector before students arrive
  • Pre-fill one sample row to demo add, enter, save in 60 seconds
  • Know your answer: 'Why does Predicted Goals have its own column?' (Answer: residual—the gap between prediction and actual is the core Week 3 concept)
  • Print paper dashboards with column headers; one per team
  • Have clipboard ready to record each team's Data Owner name
  • *This is not a filing task—it is a mathematical prerequisite. Every blank row in the dashboard means missing data for Week 3 analysis. Week 1 data does not count for standings, making it the ideal practice window for building the habit.*
Students Produce
  • Paper dashboard labeled with all 11 column headers and purpose
  • Team agreement on Data Owner role and name
  • Confirmation of three-step workflow: Load → Enter → Save
  • First match row completed (paper) before leaving match table
Reference
COLUMN REFERENCE
1: Date | 2: Match # | 3–6: S, P, E, T (independent vars) | 7: Opponent | 8: Score (outcome) | 9: Predicted Goals (residual source) | 10: W/L/D | 11: Notes
COMPLETION STANDARD
Every cell filled same day. Estimates with Notes flags are acceptable; blanks are not.
Timing
0:00Context: Why data mattersConnect first bot drive to need for evidence. Ask: 'What happened in that drive you'd want to remember in Week 3?' Listen for 2–3 responses about speed, control, scoring. Frame observations as data.
0:03Column walkthroughShow table on screen. Name each column and its purpose in one sentence. After columns 3–6, ask: 'Why does Predicted Goals get its own column?' (Probe for: 'it's what we expected.' Don't explain—they figure it out Week 3.)
0:07Completion standardState rule non-negotiably: every cell filled same day, no blanks. Ask: 'What happens if you have five blank rows?' (Encourage them to name it: missing data, can't compare.) Do not answer for them.
0:10Data Owner assignmentTeams pick one person, 60 seconds. Circulate and record names. If team cannot agree, assign to captain: 'You're the Data Owner now; reassign after if you need.' Do not linger.
0:12Live demo: digital dashboardSmooth, fast. Click +Add row, fill one sample row (date, match #1, stats, opponent, score, prediction, W/L/D), click Save. Point to confirmation. Say: 'Paper is capture (60 sec at table). Digital is the real record (Load, Enter, Save within 10 min).'
0:14Exit check (non-optional)Ask each team: 'Who is your Data Owner?' Then: 'What are the three steps after every match?' Wait for Load, Enter, Save. If any team falters, have another team model it. Do not move on until all teams confirm.
Say This
Open
You just drove your bot for the first time. Before we move on, what's one thing that happened in that drive you'd want to remember in three weeks when you're deciding whether to change your stats? [Pause 7 sec.]
Key Point
Every number you don't write down today is a decision you can't make in Week 3. That's exactly what this dashboard is for.
On Predicted Goals
The gap between what you predicted and what actually happened—that gap has a name in math. You'll figure out what it's called in Week 3. For now: write it down every match.
On Completion
Here is the standard. It is not negotiable. By the end of each match day, every cell in that row is filled. If you don't have an exact number, write your best estimate and flag it in Notes. Blank cells mean the data is gone—and we cannot recover missing data.
Not This
Don't skip Week 1 entries because they don't count for standings. The teams that build this habit in Week 1 will be ahead in Week 3 when we start using this data mathematically.
Watch For → Redirect
Student asks what to do if they miss data after a matchWrite your best estimate in the cell and note it's estimated in Notes. An estimate we can flag is more useful than nothing.
Team cannot agree on a Data OwnerAssign to team captain directly: 'You're the Data Owner for now. You can reassign after the session—just not right now.' Move on immediately.
Student finishes early and goes off-taskCheck your column order against the reference on screen. Then explain to your teammate: what is Predicted Goals used for and why is it separate from Score?
Team questions why Week 1 data is required if it doesn't count for standingsYour Week 3 algebra uses all 20 rows. Teams that skip Week 1 will have 8 fewer data points. The habit is the point.
Digital dashboard won't load or has a technical issueDo not troubleshoot live. Say: 'Paper dashboards are the real record. Digital entry can happen next session. Keep going.' Note issue for tech support.
Hard Stop Exit check is non-optional. Every team must state their Data Owner name and confirm the three-step workflow (Load, Enter, Save) out loud before moving to the next session.
