3 of 7
50 Min · Challenge Block · Day 5 · AM
Follows Two-Formula Warm-Up
MATH: S + P + E + T = 20 · All four formula outputs required · = sign means balance, not result signal
Before You Start
  • Budget Constraint worksheets at every table — one per team member
  • Teams of 3, paper + data dashboard open
  • Four formulas visible on board or wall (from warm-up)
  • Personal stat cards face-down — not in use until Season 1
  • The misconception correction at min 45 is not optional — do not skip it.
Students Produce
  • 3 hypothetical loadouts (none matching personal card)
  • All four formula outputs calculated for each loadout
  • Trade-off question answered: names the stat and its changed output
  • Misconception correction heard: equals sign = balance, not result signal
The Balance Check
Equals sign means
Not
Both sides weigh the same
"Answer goes on the right"
+2 to Speed = −2 from somewhere
+2 to Speed = total becomes 22
Draw the scale: left side S+P+E+T, right side 20. Show what happens when S grows by 2. The left side is heavier. Something must decrease.
Timing
0–5Launch Hand out worksheets. "Design 3 hypothetical loadouts — none can match your personal stat card. Calculate all four formula outputs for each. 40 minutes. Go."
5–30Loadouts 1–2 Circulate. If stuck on Loadout 2: "What is the smallest change you could make to Loadout 1 that gives a different Speed output? That's Loadout 2." Check all four outputs are calculated.
30–45Trade-Off Q "If Speed = 8, what are you giving up? Name the stat, the reduction in points, and what that does to the formula output. Show every number." Not "12 points" — which stat, what output?
45–50Misconception Call the full group. Draw the scale: S+P+E+T on left, 20 on right. "Both sides weigh the same. +2 to Speed = −2 from somewhere else." Have teams verify their loadouts sum to 20.
50Hard Stop Every team has 3 loadouts with all four formula outputs complete. Verify before Cold Recall begins.
Say This
If stuck on L2
"What is the smallest change to Loadout 1 that gives a different Speed output? Make that Loadout 2."
Trade-off Q
"If Speed equals 8, what are you giving up? Not 12 points — which stat absorbs the reduction, by how much, and what happens to that output?"
Misconception
"The equals sign says both sides weigh the same. Not: the answer is coming. It says: both sides are equal."
Not this
Don't address the equals sign confusion mid-session. Note it and save it for minute 45. One correction at the right time beats three interruptions.
Watch For → Redirect
Copying personal stat card "None of the three can match your card. Change at least one number."
Only Speed output filled in "All four. Power, Turning, Endurance — show the number for each."
Stat = 0 Let it stand. Formula output will be 0. Valid design choice with visible consequence.
Finishes early "Compare Power output across all three loadouts. What's the range? What causes the difference?"
Trade-off = "12 points" "Which stat takes the 12? What does that do to the output? Name the number."
Loadout totals ≠ 20 Hold them at the misconception correction. Fix the arithmetic before they move on.
Hard StopEvery team has 3 loadouts with all four formula outputs complete and verified to sum to 20.
