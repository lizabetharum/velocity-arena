Reveal Script
Say "Three columns. Wins and Goals Scored work how you'd expect. Best Prediction is different. It tracks how close your pre-match prediction was to your actual result. You can be 0-and-3 on wins and lead Best Prediction if your model is precise. That column matters for Season 2 planning."
Pause Before Best Prediction column: "Two teams on this board have the same wins. One predicted their goal total within one. The other didn't. Those two teams are not in the same position for Season 2 planning — even if their win totals match."
Open Lab — Watch For
Team trying to change stat card allocation. "Stat card is fixed. Code adjustments only — you can test what a different Speed value does, but the card stays the same."
Team not engaging — sitting quietly. Wait 3 minutes. Emotional processing is not linear. Then: "What are you looking at?" — not "What are you doing?" Opens a door without pressure.
Best Prediction = 0 (exact prediction). Encourage it: "Your model was exact. What does that tell you about your allocation?" Turn the celebration into an analytical insight.
Do Not: tell any team what to adjust. "You might want to look at Endurance" is a direction disguised as suggestion. Ask "What surprised you in Round 1?" and wait.
Key Facilitation Move

The room will read Wins first. Redirect to Best Prediction before the emotional response to the win/loss column locks in. Name the 0-win team that leads Best Prediction — read their entry out loud. That is the mathematical accuracy payoff the whole block built toward.
