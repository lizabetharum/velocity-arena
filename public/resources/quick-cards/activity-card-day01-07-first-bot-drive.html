First Bot Drive
7 of 9
Day 1 · Match-Build-Make · 80 min
Follows Speed Stat Challenge
MATH: motor power = (Speed × 100) ÷ 20  ·  Each Speed point = 5% motor power  ·  Does doubling Speed double distance?
Before You Start
  • micro:bit paired to Cutebot Pro and tested
  • Cutebot Pro trained to sense black lines
  • MakeCode open — new project projected on screen
  • Coordinate grid on floor, (0,0) taped, bot front faces positive x
  • Data dashboard open — confirm live row entry works
  • Tape or marker on hand to mark stopping points
  • Demo runs ~20 min. Students do not touch hardware until You Do.
Students Produce
  • Claim sentence written: "Doubling Speed did / did not double our distance. That is ___ times as far."
  • At least one complete row entered in the data dashboard (Speed value, Run 1 distance, Run 2 distance)
Demo Run Key
Speed = 630% motor power. Record distance → Run 1.
Speed = 1260% motor power. Record distance → Run 2. Students divide Run 2 ÷ Run 1.
Claim"Doubling Speed did / did not double our distance. That is ___ times as far."
Motor power doubles — real-world distance may not. Friction and surface are variables. That gap is the insight.
Timing
0:00Hardware setupHold up micro:bit and Cutebot Pro together. Slide micro:bit in edge-first slowly. Show power switch. Point to LED matrix — wait until every student confirms they can see it.
0:03MakeCode demoPoint to SPEED variable on screen. "SPEED equals 6. Formula multiplies by 100 ÷ 20 = 30% motor power. You calculated that. Now the formula does it." Click Download. Let class hear motors activate.
0:08Run 1 — Speed 6Place bot at (0,0), front facing positive x — make placement visible. Let it run. Don't narrate. Mark stop. Read coordinate. Enter row in dashboard live in front of class.
0:13Run 2 — Speed 12Return to (0,0), make reset visible. Change SPEED to 12 in MakeCode. "One change. Formula recalculates automatically." Flash. Run. Mark. Measure. Enter second row.
0:18Claim sentenceWrite the claim sentence on the board exactly as shown. "Divide Run 2 by Run 1. Don't guess." Students fill in their own numbers.
0:20You DoTeams run with their stat card Speed value. Every team enters at least one dashboard row before block ends.
Say This
Hardware
"The micro:bit slots in right here. Red LED on the micro:bit + 4 blue LEDs on the back — that means it's ready."
Formula
"SPEED equals 6. Multiply by 100, divide by 20 — that's 30% motor power. You calculated that on your worksheet. The code does it automatically now."
Reset
"Starting point: zero, zero. Front of the bot faces positive x. Same start, every run."
Claim
"Doubling our Speed did / did not double our distance. Speed = 6 gave ___ cm. Speed = 12 gave ___ cm. That is ___ times as far."
Not this
Don't orient MakeCode beyond the three blocks in use. Don't open Endurance/Turning/Power. Don't ask "Did it double?" — that's the students' question to answer.
Watch For → Redirect
Student asks how other MakeCode blocks work"Today we use three blocks." Move on. Full MakeCode orientation is not Day 1 content.
Team can't get bot to moveCheck: micro:bit fully seated? Battery secure? New code downloaded with bot turned off? Walk through these in order.
Team's distances don't follow the patternDon't fix it for them. "Write your numbers. We're looking at what the data shows — not what it should show."
Team tries to change Speed beyond their stat card value"The stat card is the record. Code the value from your card." They can experiment with other values to observe — but the card doesn't change.
Hard Stop Every team must have at least one complete row in the data dashboard before leaving this block. Stay until they enter it.
