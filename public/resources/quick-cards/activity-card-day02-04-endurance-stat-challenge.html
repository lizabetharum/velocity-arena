MATH: P(t) = 100 – (20–E) × 1.5 × t  ·  Slope = –(20–E)×1.5 = decay rate  ·  Each Endurance pt changes slope by 1.5%/min
Before You Start
  • Worksheets printed — one per student
  • Formula on board: P(t) = 100 – (20–E) × 1.5 × t
  • Answer key in hand (see table)
  • Projector ready for graph demo at 0:25
  • The 7 phases run tight. Call each transition out loud. Students who hit the floor (E≤2) have a clamped P — address it as a feature, not an error.
Students Produce
  • Completed worksheet: P(t) calculations for E=6 (worked), E=4 (scaffolded), E=2 (independent)
  • Graph plotted for their own Endurance allocation
  • Endurance value confirmed on stat card

Slope-intercept form: P = –(20–E)×1.5 · t + 100. The coefficient of t is the decay rate — the slope of the line. Each Endurance point changes that slope by 1.5%/min.
Timing — 7 Phases
0:05Worked exampleE=6 on the board together. Plug in: (20–6)=14. 14×1.5=21. P(t) = 100 – 21t. Calculate P(1)=79, P(2)=58, P(3)=37. Students watch only.
5:15ScaffoldedE=4. Students fill in blanks with your guidance. "What is 20 minus 4?" Call on volunteers. Don't give answers unprompted.
15:25IndependentE=2. Students work solo. Circulate. Don't answer "Is this right?" — redirect to the formula. Students check each other at end of this phase.
25:35Graph own allocationStudents calculate P(0), P(1), P(2), P(3) for their own Endurance value and plot on the worksheet graph.
35:40Two-team compareEach team shares P(3) with one adjacent team. "Which bot has more power left at 3 minutes? By how much?" Pairs discuss.
40:47SynthesisWrite on board: P = –(20–E)×1.5·t + 100. "The slope of this line is the decay rate. What changes when E goes up?" Students answer: slope gets less steep — slower decay.
47:50Stat card entryEvery team confirms their Endurance on their stat card matches the value they graphed. No blanks before leaving.
Say This
Worked ex.
"E equals 6. Step 1: 20 minus 6 equals 14. That's the decay rate — how many percent per minute. Step 2: multiply by 1.5. Step 3: subtract from 100. We have a formula."
Slope
"Look at your graph. That line is P = negative-something times t plus 100. The negative-something is the slope. Higher Endurance means a less steep slope — slower decay."
Floor
"If your formula gives a number below 0 at t=3, that means the bot is dead. But in the actual match code, we set a floor. The bot doesn't stop — it crawls."
Not this
Don't answer "Is this right?" during independent work — redirect to the formula. Don't skip the synthesis. The slope-intercept connection is the algebraic payoff of the whole block.
Watch For → Redirect
Student multiplies (20–E) incorrectly — order of operations error"Do the parentheses first. What is 20 minus your E value?" Get that number. Then multiply. One step at a time.
Student's graph line goes below zero — P is negative"That means the bot would be out of battery. In the match, the code floors it so the bot doesn't stop. For now, extend to 0 and draw a dashed line after that."
Student finishes early and disengages"Calculate for E=1 and E=20. What do those lines look like?" Keeps the math going without off-script content.
Phase runs over timeCall the transition even if not every student is done. "Pause where you are. We're moving to the next step." Catch-up happens during the graph phase.
Hard Stop Every team writes their Endurance value on the stat card before leaving. Confirm every card.
