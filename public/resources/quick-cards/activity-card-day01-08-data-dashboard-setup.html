Data Dashboard Setup
8 of 9
Day 1 · Match-Build-Make · 15 min
Follows First Bot Drive
PURPOSE: Establish the data collection habit and assign ownership before the first match. Paper captures. Digital commits. The habit built in Week 1 determines data quality all season.
Before You Start
  • Digital dashboard projected on screen and accessible on all team devices
  • Paper dashboard copies ready — 1 per team
  • Column reference visible on screen while students label their own dashboards
  • Have the "+ Add row" button and Save button ready to demo live.
Students Produce
  • First complete row in the team's data dashboard with all required fields filled
  • Dashboard confirmed live (facilitator verifies row entry before block ends)

Paper captures. Digital commits. Paper row filled at the table immediately after each match. Digital entered within 10 minutes of match block ending.
Timing
0:00PurposeExplain what the dashboard is for in 2 minutes. Don't over-talk it. Move quickly to walking through columns together.
0:02Walk columnsWalk through each column live. Students write as you speak. One column per sentence — don't elaborate. Students label their own dashboards as you go.
0:06Set the standard"By the end of each match day, every cell in that row is filled. Not tomorrow. Not the next day. That match day." Make the completion rule specific and non-negotiable.
0:12Assign data owner"One person is responsible for entering data after each match. Decide who that is right now." Give 60 seconds. Note who takes the role — follow up at Week 4 checkpoint.
0:13Live demoShow digital dashboard on projector. Click "+ Add row," fill in one sample row, click Save. Point to the confirmation. "Load, enter, save. Three clicks after every match."
0:15Close"Week 1 is practice — your data still goes in every row but doesn't count for standings. Starting Week 2, everything counts." Move to Open Lab.
Say This
Standard
"Here is the standard: by the end of each match day, every cell in that row is filled. Not tomorrow. Not the next day. That match day. If you don't have a number, write your best estimate and flag it in Notes."
Paper vs digital
"Paper is your match-day capture tool. The second a match ends, your Data Owner fills in the paper row. Within 10 minutes, open the digital dashboard and transfer it. Then hit Save."
Data owner
"One person is responsible for entering data after each match. Decide who that is right now."
Close
"Week 1 is practice. Starting Week 2, everything counts. The teams who built the habit in Week 1 will be ahead."
Watch For → Redirect
Team asks what to do if they miss data"Write your best estimate in the cell and note it's estimated in Notes. Don't leave it blank."
Team can't agree on a data ownerAssign it to the team captain. "You can reassign after the session — just not right now."
Student finishes early and goes off-taskAsk them to double-check column order against the reference on screen. Keeps them focused without adding a new task.
Team says they'll do the digital later"Within 10 minutes of each match block. Not later. Show me your data owner's name."
Hard Stop Every team must have a named Data Owner before leaving this block. Note each name — you will follow up with them after Match 1.
