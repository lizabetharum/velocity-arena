PURPOSE: 5 questions, 10 min silent, no notes · Diagnostic for facilitation priority — not a grade
Before You Start
  • Write all 5 questions on the board before students arrive — do not display until clock starts
  • Blank paper at every seat — no worksheets, no stat cards on the table
  • No devices during the 10-minute window
  • This is a diagnostic for you, not a grade for them. Use error patterns to set facilitation priorities for the rest of the day.
Students Produce
  • Written answers to 5 recall questions (not collected, not graded)
  • Participated in share-out: heard correct answers and common errors

Timing
0–2Launch "Not a test. Not graded. Five questions, ten minutes, no notes, no devices. Write answers. Skip what you don't know." Reveal questions. Start timer. Say nothing else.
2–10Silent Work Circulate and read — do not react. Note which questions draw the most blank paper. Do not start share-out before 10 min even if room finishes early.
10–18Share-Out All 5 questions, 90 sec each. Call correct answer first, then wrong answers. When wrong: "Can someone show where the calculation went off?" Don't correct yourself — ask the room.
18–20Hard Stop Note (on paper, not board) which questions had most errors — that is your facilitation priority for the Budget Constraint and afternoon simulation.
Say This
Launch
"This is not a test. I'm not grading it. I'm using this to figure out what I need to teach differently — not what you got wrong. Five questions. Ten minutes. Work alone."
During silence
[If asked to clarify:] "Your best attempt." Do not clarify vocabulary or hint at a method.
Share-out
"[Name] got 3 instead of 30. Can someone show where the calculation went off?"
Not this
Don't re-teach during share-out. You are surfacing errors and confirming answers. Re-teaching happens in Open Lab or at the start of the Budget Constraint.
Watch For → Redirect
Q1: gets 1/30 or 0.033 Division direction reversed. Flag — same reversal will appear in afternoon cm/s calculations.
Q2: adds instead of scales Additive reasoning on multiplicative relationship. Speed formula calculations may be procedural without structure.
Q4: reports 65% not 35% Misreads "unallocated" as "allocated." Most direct warm-up for Budget Constraint — multiple errors here = extra scaffolding needed.
Q5: "yes it doubles" Proportional default. Re-name at start of simulation: "Power is the only stat where doubling ≠ double output."
