Opening Activity — Week 2
MATH: Speed% = (S ÷ 20) × 100 · Three-person relay: substitute → divide → multiply · Card B runs the formula backwards
Before You Start
  • Formula on board before students enter: motor power = (? / 20) × 100 — write the ? exactly, do not pre-fill
  • Elimination bracket drawn: 6–7 slots R1, 4 advance to R2, 2 to Final
  • One marker at each team's board station — teams cannot share
  • Round values on your notecard: R1: S=8 (40%), R2: S=5 (25%), R3: S=13 (65%)
  • Card A and Card B printed and answers confirmed
  • Timer visible on projector
Students Produce
  • Formula applied at the board at least once per student
  • Card A completed independently (eliminated after R1/R2)
  • Card B attempted: formula run backwards — find Speed from motor power %

Timing
0–2Launch "Three steps, three people. P1 → P2 → P3. Each writes one step. Call answer as a %. Top 4 go to R2. Speed = 8. Go." Step back immediately.
2–7Round 1 Watch all boards. Call finish order live. Write names on bracket. Wrong answer: "Not yet. Keep going." Do not coach.
7–8Bracket Update "Where did it break for teams that got 4,000%?" One answer. Cross out eliminated. Hand Card A. Start R2 immediately.
8–12Round 2 Rotate: P2 first, P3 second, P1 closes. "First 2 correct go to Final. Speed = 5. Go." Eliminated R1 teams on Card A — verify working, not watching.
12–14Bracket Update Post two finalists. "Card B — formula backwards." Hand Card B to R2 eliminated. Card A teams finish then move to Card B.
14–18Final Rotate: P3 first, P1 second, P2 closes. Speed = 13. Non-finalists on Card B. Scan room — no passive watching.
18–20Bridge Reveal Card B answer. "What you just did is algebra — you ran the formula backwards." Sit them down. Move directly to Challenge Block.
Say This
Launch
"Week 2 starts right now. Stand up and get to the whiteboard."
Wrong answer
"Not yet. Keep going."
After R1 error
"Where did it break for teams that got 4,000%?"
Bridge
"What you just did on Card B is algebra — you ran the formula backwards. That's today's Challenge Block."
Not this
Don't explain the step breakdown before R1. Don't count down the timer. Don't coach step errors during the round.
Watch For → Redirect
Decimal error: "4,000%" Don't intervene. The room's reaction teaches the rule. After the round: "What step broke the chain?"
Teams argue about step split Let it happen. 30 sec of argument is faster than 2 min of explanation.
Eliminated teams watching Final "Eyes on Card B." One phrase. Move on.
Both finalists call at same time, one wrong Don't award the round. Both teams keep working until one is clearly correct.
