PURPOSE: Open Lab (0–40 min): compare loadouts vs predictions · Closing Ritual (40–60 min): Week 1 journal — personal, not mathematical
Before You Start
  • Simulation data still visible on dashboards — do not reset
  • Three morning loadouts still on paper at each table
  • Journal prompt written on board or as handout — stage before Open Lab close
  • Physical journals or blank paper ready before the Open Lab close
  • Prompt: "What is one thing you are good at that most people do not know about? Be specific. Name the thing. Give a real example from your own life."
Students Produce
  • Identified which loadout best matched formula predictions
  • Named the formula output that was most accurate or most wrong
  • Written journal entry (Week 1 closing prompt)
  • Shared journal entry (or chose to pass)

Timing
0–2OL Launch "Look at your simulation data. Which loadout matched your predictions best? Try the ones you didn't run. Go." Step back immediately.
2–30Circulate Available, not directive. Ask "What are you trying?" Teams that only tested one loadout: guide toward a second. Note teams that have not compared yet by minute 20.
3010-Min Warning Call it once. Note teams that haven't compared a second loadout yet — they need to run one comparison before devices close.
35–40OL Close "Devices down." Bridge sentence only — do not summarize the day. The journal prompt is already on the board.
40–45Journal Launch Read the prompt once. "5 minutes to write. You don't have to share. Be specific — name the thing, give a real example." Set timer. Go quiet.
45–58Sharing Voluntary share. No pressure. Go around — offer every camper the chance. "Would you like to share?" "Pass" is a complete answer. Don't comment evaluatively.
58–60Close One closing sentence. That's Day 5 and Week 1. No summary, no preview of Week 2.

Not this
Don't summarize the day or preview Week 2 at the close. The journal entry is the last word. Let it be.
Watch For → Redirect
Only tested 1 loadout "Which one did you not run in simulation? Program it now. You need the comparison."
Designing new 4th loadout "Compare what you have first. New designs after the comparison."
Can't identify best loadout "Look at the gap sentence for each. Which gap is smallest? That's the closest match."
Journal: too general Don't prompt during the 5 min. After: "What's a real example from your life?"
Refuses to share "Pass is a complete answer." Move to the next camper without comment.
OL runs long Call "Devices down" at 0:35 regardless of where teams are. The journal has its own 20 minutes.
