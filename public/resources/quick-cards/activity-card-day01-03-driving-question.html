Driving Question Reveal + Team Formation
3 of 8
Day 1 · Hook Block · 10 min
Follows Mirror Pairs
MATH:
Before You Start
  • One large poster — covered or face-down — positioned where entire room can read it
  • Teams pre-assigned and recorded; seats arranged in groups of three, tagged by team number
  • Index cards or scrap paper for exit reflection — one per student
  • Close this script. Imagine you are about to reveal something that will matter to these students for 20 days. What would you want them to feel in the first 30 seconds? Write two sentences.
Students Produce
  • Verbal restatement of driving question to a partner
  • One thing they already know how to do
  • One thing they have never tried before
  • Written paraphrase of driving question in their own words
Timing
0:00–0:02Context ActivationPrime thinking: what makes a team actually win, not get lucky?
0:02–0:04Driving Question RevealUncover poster. Read slowly. Hold 10 seconds of silence. Partner comprehension check.
0:04–0:09Team FormationCall names at steady pace. Seat teams. Intro task: one thing you know, one thing you haven't tried.
0:09–0:10Closing ConsolidationName learning goal. Restate driving question. Exit reflection: paraphrase in your own words.
Say This
Open
Think about the last time you saw a team that just kept winning. Not getting lucky, actually winning. What made them different from the team that lost?
Reveal
How can you use data, equations, and code to build a robot athlete that wins on math, not luck?
Not This
You two will work great together. Let me explain the question. Let me preview Day 2.
Redirect
Teams are set. You'll understand why later.
Watch For → Redirect
Student lobbies to change teamsTeams are set. You'll understand why later. Don't negotiate or explain.
Student looks isolated during introductionsWalk over. Ask directly: 'What's one thing you know how to do?' Give them a clear entry point.
Energy drops after the revealThis is expected. The question is big. Let the quiet be productive. Do not pump energy artificially.
Students ask about robots right awayGood questions. Hold them. We start tomorrow. Do not preview Day 2 content.
Room is chaotic during team formationSlow your pace. Call one name at a time. Wait for seating before next name. Do not raise your voice.
Student writes nothing or copies verbatim on exit reflectionNot graded. Diagnostic only. Direct check-in before first math session on Day 2.
Hard Stop Hold silence for 10 seconds after driving question reveal. Do not fill it, explain, or smile reassuringly.
