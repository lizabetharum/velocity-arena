Before Session
  • Rivalry Cards still on wall from Day 8 — do not remove them
  • Sticky notes + markers at every table (at least 2 notes per team)
  • Dashboard accessible from every seat
  • Know which team sent which card (for dispute resolution)
Purpose (LO 7.2)

Rivalry Cards introduced a claim. This activity forces a data-grounded response. "That's wrong" without opening the dashboard is not a response. Opening the dashboard, finding the number, and writing the correction is.

The verification habit — checking a stat before accepting or rejecting a claim — is the same habit needed for post-match analysis.

Students Produce
  • Sticky note correction (if number was wrong): team name + cited stat + correct value
  • One-sentence plan (if number was right): what to do about it before Match Day 3

If Most Cards Were Accurate

Name it: "Most of these numbers checked out. That means the people who wrote these cards were using the actual data." Then move. Do not let the activity feel anticlimactic — accuracy is a win.

Launch Script
Say "The card on your table is the one your rival sent you. Read it. One rule: before you respond to anything on that card, you open your dashboard and check the number they cited. Not your memory — the actual dashboard entry."
Say "Two outcomes. If their number is wrong: write the correct value on a sticky note, put your team name on it, and post it on their card on the wall. If their number is right: write one sentence about what you plan to do about it before Match Day 3. You have 10 minutes."
Watch For
Team accepts trash talk without checking dashboard. "Open the dashboard. Find the exact number they cited. Then decide if it's right." No plan or correction until they have looked at the data.
Team wants to confront the rival directly. "The card goes on their wall card — not to them directly." The rivalry is mediated through data, not confrontation.
Team cannot find the stat in their dashboard. "Is that value from Day 7 or Day 8?" If missing: write "no data recorded" on the sticky. That gap is its own signal.
Sticky note missing one of the three required elements. Team name + cited stat + corrected value. All three. If missing any, they add before posting.
Hard Stop9:20 — Transition to Formula Relay. One finding named, then move.
