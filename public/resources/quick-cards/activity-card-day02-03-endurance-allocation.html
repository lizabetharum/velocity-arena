MATH: Speed + Endurance ≤ 18  ·  Must leave ≥ 2 pts for Turning + Power (Days 3–4)
Before You Start
  • Stat cards distributed — Speed value already filled in
  • Constraint on board: Speed + Endurance ≤ 18
  • Pencils in hand — pens are fine, cards are binding
  • 90-second timer ready
  • This block is 3 minutes. Don't pad it. The constraint and the timer are the whole structure.
Students Produce
  • Endurance value written on stat card — binding from the moment it is written
The Constraint
Speed + Endurance ≤ 18
Turning and Power haven't been introduced yet — but they're coming (Days 3 and 4). Students must leave at least 2 points for those two stats.
Valid: Speed 8 + Endurance 9 = 17 ✓
Invalid: Speed 8 + Endurance 11 = 19 ✗
Edge: Endurance 0 = bot dies at ~30s
Timing
0:00State the rule"One constraint before you write: Speed plus Endurance cannot exceed 18. You must leave at least 2 points for Turning and Power — those come later this week."
0:20Start timer"You have 90 seconds. Decide your Endurance value and write it on your stat card. The card is binding the moment the number is on it." Start the visible timer.
1:5010-second call"10 seconds." Let the timer do the work. Don't repeat the rules.
2:00Pencils down"Pencils down. The card is final." Do a quick visual scan — every card should have a number.
2:30Bridge"Hold that number. In the next block you'll see exactly what it does to your bot." No discussion. Move immediately to Endurance Stat Challenge.
3:00Hard stopEvery student writes a value before the group moves on. Don't leave without confirming every card has a number.
Say This
Rule
"Speed plus Endurance must be 18 or less. Add your Speed value to whatever you're thinking for Endurance. Is the sum 18 or less? Good. Write it."
Timer
"90 seconds. Write a number. The card is binding the moment the ink is on it."
After
"You'll see why this number matters in the next block. Hold it in your head."
Not this
Don't let students revise after writing. Don't explain Turning or Power yet. Don't debate whether a specific Endurance value is "good" — that's what the stat challenge is for.
Watch For → Redirect
Student's Speed + Endurance exceeds 18"Add those two numbers. Is the sum 18 or less?" Point to the board. Wait. Don't give them the answer.
Student writes Endurance = 0"Endurance of zero means your bot runs full speed for about 30 seconds, then stops. Is that your choice?" Let them decide — it's a valid (and interesting) data point.
Student asks "What's the best Endurance?""That's what the next block figures out. Right now, pick a number." Move on.
Student hasn't written anything at 2:00Walk to their seat. "Pick a number, any number that satisfies the constraint. You can analyze it after. Write it now."
Hard Stop Every student writes an Endurance value on the stat card before the group moves on. No blanks.
