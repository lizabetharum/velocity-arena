Track Distinction

Track A (Data): read Season 1 dashboard → identify one stat to change → write hypothesis with data citation.

Track B (Bot Lab): write hypothesis FIRST, then test. Running the bot and then writing a hypothesis to match what you saw is post-hoc rationalization — not Season 2 planning. One variable at a time.

Both tracks end with a written hypothesis. Season 2 reallocation is permanent.

Students Produce
  • One written Season 2 hypothesis citing specific Season 1 data
  • Track B: hypothesis written before test run + test result added as confirming or contradicting evidence
  • Pair-share: adjacent team identifies stat change + data citation from your hypothesis

Strong vs. Weak Hypothesis

Strong: "If I increase Speed from 6 to 9, my goals/min will increase because in rounds against Speed ≤5 I won 3 of 3, but against Speed ≥9 I lost 4 of 4."

Weak: "If I increase Speed, I'll win more because I felt slow." (Feeling may be correct — hypothesis is not usable without data.)

Launch Script
Say "Two options. Track A is data: look at your Season 1 dashboard, pick one stat you want to change, and write a hypothesis. Your hypothesis has to include a real number from your dashboard — not a feeling about how your matches went."
Say "Track B is bot testing: you try out the change before you write your hypothesis. But Track B only counts as thinking if you write the hypothesis first. If you run the bot, see what happens, and then write a hypothesis to match it — that's not a plan. That's working backwards. Write the hypothesis first, then test it."
Say "Both tracks finish with a written hypothesis. Season 2 reallocation is permanent. This is your last chance to experiment without it counting. Go."
Watch For
Track A: hypothesis changes two stats simultaneously. "Pick the one stat your data most directly supports. You can plan the second change after you see how the first performs."
Track A: hypothesis cites win % without specifying opponent stats. "Of the 3 matches you lost, what was your opponent's dominant stat? What does that tell you about which of your stats needs to change?" W/L by stat matchup column has the answer.
Track B: hypothesis contradicted by test — team wants to rewrite hypothesis. "Hypothesis stays as written. Add the test result as contradicting evidence. A disproven hypothesis with recorded data is more analytically useful than a convenient hypothesis that was never tested."
Team asks: "Should we increase Speed or Endurance?" "What does your W/L by stat matchup column show?" Point to the dashboard. Do not answer the question.
