Program Endurance Into Your Bot
5 of 8
Day 2 · Make-Build-Make · 65 min
Follows Endurance Stat Challenge
MATH: P(t) = 100 – decayRate × t ÷ 40  ·  decayRate = 20–E  ·  wheelSpeed = motorPower × currentPower ÷ 100  ·  Floor: currentPower ≥ 20
Before You Start
  • Computers open — MakeCode ready
  • Floor clear — each team needs 2m straight run
  • Tape measures or meter sticks — one per team
  • Masking tape for 4 floor markers per team
  • 65-minute timer set. Call marks at 0:25, 0:30, 0:43, 0:55, 1:02
  • Frame the gap before the test run or you will spend the last 15 minutes reassuring teams they didn't break anything. The gap is the point.
Students Produce
  • Working Endurance decay code in MakeCode (loop with floor at currentPower = 20)
  • Gap sentence written: "Formula predicted ___ cm. Bot did ___ cm. Gap is ___ cm."
  • Two test distances (first 30s and last 30s) + gap value entered in data dashboard
Code Variables
motorPower= SPEED × 100 ÷ 20 (base wheel speed) decayRate= 20 – ENDURANCE (calculated once) currentPower= 100 – decayRate × t ÷ 40 (floor: 20) wheelSpeed= currentPower × motorPower ÷ 100
Formula adapted for MakeCode: 1.5 → 3÷2; time in seconds not minutes; t÷40 collapses both. Prediction is unchanged — only the form changes.
Floor: if currentPower < 20, set to 20. Bot crawls — it does not stop.
Timing
0:07Code walkthroughBoard: write the 3 formula fixes. (1) 1.5 → multiply by 3, divide by 2. (2) Time in seconds, not minutes. (3) Final form: P(t) = 100 – decayRate × t ÷ 40. Explain each fix — don't skip. Show worked example (Speed=7, E=3) live.
0:25Teams codeTeams open MakeCode and translate their own stat values. Circulate. Watch for common errors (see Watch For). Don't write code for any team — errors are data.
0:30Test prep"Stop coding. Mark start position with tape. Assign: one timer-caller, one floor-marker." Teams place bot at start and confirm clear lane.
0:432-min test run"On my mark — go." Call 30s and 90s for the whole room. Floor-markers tape at 30s and 90s. At 120s: "Stop." Mark final position. You call time for everyone.
0:55Record + gap sentenceMeasure: Distance 1 (start→30s mark), Distance 2 (90s→120s mark). Calculate formula prediction for last 30s. Write: "Formula predicted ___ cm. Bot did ___ cm. Gap is ___ cm."
1:02Dashboard entryBoth distances + gap → dashboard. Notes column for gap explanation. No blanks.
1:05Whole-room shareOne team (mid-range gap) reads their gap sentence aloud. Close: "That's a complete scientific statement. The formula was right. The gap is information."
Say This
Gap frame
"Before you run: the formula will predict a distance. The bot will do something slightly different. That gap is not a mistake. It's the most interesting thing you'll measure today."
Launch
"On my mark, start your bots. Timer-callers: announce 30 seconds, 90 seconds, and 120 seconds out loud. Floor-markers: tape at those times. Ready? Go."
Gap sentence
"The formula predicted ___ cm. The bot did ___ cm. The gap is ___ cm — probably because ___."
Not this
Don't write or fix code for any team — a wrong formula produces a larger gap, which is still valid data. Don't rank gaps as good or bad. Don't skip the gap sentence even if time is short.
Watch For → Redirect
Team hasn't started coding at 0:15"What's your Endurance value?" Get the number. "What's 20 minus that?" Get the decay rate. "Open MakeCode. Write decayRate equals that number." Give one step. Walk away.
Bot speeds up instead of slowing downTeam added decay rate instead of subtracting. Ask after run: "What was your 1-minute motor speed in the code?" If above starting speed, the sign is wrong. Note it in the gap sentence.
Bot doesn't visibly slow down"Did it finish 2 minutes?" Yes → "Measure both distances. The numbers will differ even if it looks the same." High E values have small but real gaps.
Team distressed by large gap (10cm+)"What did I say about a large gap?" Redirect to their gap sentence: "What does the formula not account for?" Friction, battery level, and motor variation are all correct.
Hard Stop Both test distances + gap sentence logged in dashboard before block ends. No blanks.
