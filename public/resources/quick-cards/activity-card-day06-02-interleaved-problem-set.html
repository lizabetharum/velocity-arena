Before You Start
  • Problem sets face-down at each table before students enter
  • No formula sheets visible anywhere in the room
  • Whiteboard blank — erase all Day 5 content
  • Timer visible on projector
  • Verification log printed or open on clipboard
  • Co-facilitator: "no rescue" rule applies to both of you until the 10-minute warning.
Students Produce

  • Facilitator initial on problem set (all 4 formulas verified correct)

Timing
0–3Launch "12 problems. Write the concept tag first — unit rate, ratio, linear decay, coordinate geometry, or proportional scaling. Then apply the formula. Flip your paper and begin."
3–37Silent Work Circuit every 7–8 min. Reading, not helping. Check: right formula? right tag? correct calc? Note errors — do not correct them.
3710-Min Warning "Ten minutes." Say it once. Do not repeat. Identify teams that will not finish — do not rescue them.

Not this

Watch For → Redirect
Looking around before flipping Wait them out. The silence is part of the task. Don't encourage.
Not flipping after 30 sec Walk over quietly, tap the table, nod at the paper. No words.
Rushing through concept tags Note it. The wrong tag with correct math tells you the transfer is procedural. Flag for Open Lab.
Won't finish — clear by min 37 Do not rescue. They carry the unfinished set into MakeCode block. No exceptions.
Team asks another team "Eyes on your own paper." Once. Don't elaborate.
Correct total but wrong individual values You check formula correctness, not sum only. Initial only if all four formulas are right.
Hard StopEvery team has a facilitator initial on their problem set. Teams without an initial do not access MakeCode.
