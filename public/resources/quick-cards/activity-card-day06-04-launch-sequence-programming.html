Before Session
  • Board: Speed × 5 = Motor Power % (stays all block)
  • Launch Sequence Test Log at every seat
  • Each team's stat card accessible
  • Teams without geometry prediction cannot begin coding — use recovery prompt
Recovery Prompt
"Write your Speed stat, multiply by 5, and estimate how long that motor power level would take to cover the distance."
Produces
  • Launch sequence coded in MakeCode (frozen at 12 min)
  • Test Log: 2 timed runs recorded
  • Predicted vs. actual delta calculated
  • One-sentence analysis written
Key Reminders
  • Code freeze = stopped, not paused
  • Same code, same position for Run 2 — no changes between
  • Failed run = "did not complete" — record it, don't re-run
Sequence
0:00Code opens"Programming one thing: starting routine — angle, direction, timing before ball contact. Not your loadout. Launch only." Ask each team: "What specifically?" If they can't answer, stop them.
0:102-min warning"Finish what you have." Watch for stat loadout open — redirect immediately.
0:12Code freezeAll MakeCode editing stops. Demo start/stop timing before any run.
0:12Run 1"One person on timer. Start when bot moves, stop at ball position. Record actual time in Row 1." Confirm no team reopened MakeCode.
0:17Run 2"Same code. Same start. Do not change anything." Verify no MakeCode changes — if a team did, data is invalid.
0:22Calculate deltaActual − predicted for each run. Positive = slower than predicted. Negative = faster. Walk stuck teams through one row together.
0:27Write sentenceMatch: "Model held because ___." Gap: "Gap happened when ___." Very different: "Two runs differed because ___." No blanks.
0:35Debrief2–3 teams read. "A gap is data — it tells you what the formula didn't account for." Close by 0:43.
0:50Log checkWalk every worksheet: 2 times + 1 sentence. No blank logs leave the room.
Say
Open"Open MakeCode. You are programming one thing today: the starting routine. This is not your loadout. That is already done. This is only the launch."
Freeze"Code freeze in two minutes. Finish what you have. You will not adjust after the runs begin."
Gap frame"A gap between your prediction and your result is data. Not a mistake. It tells you something your formula did not account for — angle, delay, friction, starting drift."
Sentence quality"'It was off' is not a sentence. 'The gap happened when the angle shifted the bot left before reaching ball position' is a sentence."
Watch For
Team opens stat loadout during coding — "That block is closed. Launch only."
Dividing by 10 instead of multiplying — point to formula on board — do not re-explain
MakeCode reopened between Run 1 and Run 2 — data is invalid — address now, not in debrief
Student without geometry prediction — use recovery prompt — no skipping to coding without a prediction anchor
Hard Stop0:50 — all Test Logs complete: 2 times + 1 sentence, no blanks
