Open Lab: Free Code Time
9 of 9
Day 1 · Open Lab · 40 min
Follows Data Dashboard Setup
PURPOSE: Students make their first self-directed decisions with the bot. No task, no deliverable. The goal is a student-initiated choice — not a discovery.
Before You Start
  • Bots on tables, powered and ready — students should not need to set up hardware
  • MakeCode accessible and open on all devices
  • Coordinate grid remains on floor from First Bot Drive — do not remove it
  • Data dashboards accessible but not required for this block
  • Do not re-explain MakeCode. Do not list things students could try. One sentence to launch, then leave them alone.
  • The absence of a task is the design. Giving one converts this into a directed activity and undermines the only thing this block produces: a student-initiated decision.
Students Produce
  • No required artifact — open exploration block
  • Any code changes, dashboard rows, or notes are at the team's discretion
Timing
0:00LaunchOne sentence only. Then move into the room. The more you say, the more you direct. A team that waits for more instructions is waiting for permission — return eye contact, give a small nod, walk away.
0:01Free explorationCirculate slowly. 2–3 minutes per table. Ask one question per stop, then move on. Ask "What are you trying?" — not "What are you doing?" The first opens a door. The second implies evaluation.
0:10Mid-checkIdentify any team with bot untouched since launch. Ask: "Tell me one thing you want the bot to do." Wait. Then: "Try that." Do not stay and supervise.
0:3010-min warningThis is a scan, not a transition. Say nothing if the room is working. Address the whole room only if someone has not engaged at all since launch.
0:33CloseCall devices down at 0:33, not 0:38. The closing ritual needs students settled and writing in under 3 minutes. Say the close while circulating — don't wait for silence first.
Say This
Launch
"Forty minutes. Bots are ready. Make them do something." [Then walk away.]
Key question
"What do you want the bot to do right now?" [Wait. Do not fill the silence. The first thing they say after 5 seconds is the answer — and it will be theirs.]
Close
"Devices down. Bots off. Before you move — take 30 seconds and think about one thing you tried today that surprised you. You'll need that thought in 2 minutes."
Not this
Never say "You could try programming X" or "What about testing Speed = 4?" or "Think about what you want for Season 1." Any suggested task converts Free Code Time into a directed activity.
Watch For → Redirect
Team waits for more instructionsReturn eye contact, give a small nod, walk away. Do not add another sentence. The silence ends when you leave.
Team tries to program greyed-out stats"Those rows are locked until each stat comes up this week. Speed is the only live variable today."
Team asks to change Speed allocation"The stat card is binding. You can change the code to see what would happen at a different value — that's an experiment. But the card stays."
Bot untouched after 10 min"Tell me one thing you want the bot to do." Wait. "Try that." Walk away. One prompt only.
Team frustrated — bot not cooperating"What did you expect it to do?" Let them answer. "Okay. Let's find out why it didn't." If they don't know, leave and return in 5 min.
Team spends whole time talking strategyLet them. Strategy conversation about Season 1 roles and stat allocations has direct curriculum value. Don't redirect to hardware.
