Pre-Task Diagnostic
5 of 9
Day 1 · Challenge Block · 15 min
Follows Stat Card Distribution
PURPOSE: Map each student's starting math knowledge before program content begins. Results change who you stand next to in the afternoon — not what you teach.
Before You Start
  • Diagnostic sheets face-down at every seat
  • Reference cards printed — 1 per student (general math contexts only, no bot content)
  • Pencils at every seat
  • Blank facilitator copy of diagnostic in your hand for circulation notes
  • Timer set — 15 min moves fast, start it before you speak
  • Do NOT hand out sheets before you finish the framing. Order matters.
  • URL: velocity-arena-gold.vercel.app/activities/diagnostic
Students Produce
  • Completed pre-task diagnostic worksheet — one per student
  • Submitted or held for facilitator scoring before Week 4 post-diagnostic
Lunch Scan — 3 Questions
Most "not yet"Which questions produced the most blanks? Adjust your circulation targets for First Bot Drive.
4+ blanksFlag these students. They need formula written step-by-step in First Bot Drive — don't announce it.
No coordinate geometryPlan 2–3 min extra setup at their table for the grid axes in First Bot Drive.
Timing
0:00Non-test framingSay the framing slowly. Make eye contact with the room, not the sheet. Do not rush to "Go." Pause after "Week 4." That sentence is the one that lands. Then hand out sheets.
0:02Individual workCirculate slowly and silently. You are reassurance, not supervision. Do not answer math questions. If asked "What does this mean?", say: "Write 'not yet' and move on." That is the full response.
0:05Mid-circulationNote which questions produce universal confusion on your blank copy. Check for pencils down within the first 3 min — that's your first intervention target.
0:121-minute warning"One minute. Finish the question you're on. If anything is blank, write 'not yet' so I have a complete picture." Do not extend time.
0:13Collect + closeCollect every sheet before students leave. Count against roster at the door. Missing sheets = gaps in your afternoon planning. Sheet first, then lunch.
Say This
Framing
"This is not a test. There is no grade. I am filling this out so I know what you already know and so I can make sure this program works for you specifically. If you see a question you do not know, write 'not yet' and move on. You will know every single thing on this paper by Week 4."
Pencil down
"If any question feels unfamiliar, write 'not yet.' That is a complete answer." [Say once, keep moving.]
1-min warning
"One minute. Finish what you're on. Anything blank — write 'not yet.'"
Close
"Pencils down. Pass your sheet to the end of the row. Every answer — including every 'not yet' — is useful to me. Enjoy the break."
Not this
Never say "It's not that hard" or "You'll be fine." Both minimize. If a student says "I don't know any of this," say: "That is exactly why I need this paper." Then leave.
Watch For → Redirect
Pencil down within first 3 minWalk close (not hovering). "If any question feels unfamiliar, write 'not yet.'" Say it once, keep moving. Don't stand there waiting.
Erasing and rewriting same answerPerformance anxiety, not confusion. Don't intervene until 8-min mark. Then: "Your first answer is fine — move to the next one."
Finishes in under 5 min"Go back and check your work. Look for any 'not yet' answers you want to try again." Don't give more questions.
Students leaning toward each other's papersWalk to the table. "This one is individual — your own answers only." Move on. No further explanation.
Student refuses — arms crossedPlace sheet in front of them. "You can write 'not yet' on every single line — that is a complete answer." Leave. Don't return until collection.
Room feels tense, not focusedWalk through slowly. Say conversationally at one table: "If anything is unfamiliar, 'not yet' is the answer." Once at normal volume resets the whole room.
Hard Stop Collect every sheet before students go to lunch. Count against roster at the door. Missing sheets mean gaps in your First Bot Drive planning that you cannot recover.
