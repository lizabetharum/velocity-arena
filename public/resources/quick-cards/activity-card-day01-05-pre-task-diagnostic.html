Follows Stat Card Distribution
MATH: Proportional reasoning, unit rate, ratio, linear decay, coordinate geometry, proportional scaling, arc approximation, slope as rate of change
Before You Start
  • Print diagnostic sheets + reference cards
  • One blank facilitator copy in your hand
  • Timer ready
  • Read framing script first. Tone is the lesson. Watch for students who shut down when seeing unfamiliar problems.
Students Produce
  • Individual written responses to 8 prior knowledge questions
  • 'Not yet' as a complete answer for unfamiliar items
  • Baseline data across math domains
Reference
Do not read questions aloud. Every response redirects to 'not yet.' Do not intervene on specific questions.
Timing

0:02–0:12Individual Work (silent)Circulate slowly. Glance at work. One pass through every seat in 10 minutes. Presence is reassurance, not supervision.
0:12–0:131-Minute WarningDo not extend time. Scripted cue only. Timer at 0:12 is cut-off.

Open

Open
You have 12 minutes. This is your own thinking — no talking, no sharing. Work silently. If anything is unclear, write 'not yet' and move on. Begin.
Not This
You'll be fine. This isn't that hard.
Watch For → Redirect
Student stops working within first 3 minutesWalk close. Say quietly: 'If any question feels unfamiliar, write not yet. That is a complete answer.' Keep moving.
Student erasing and rewriting the same answerAt 8-minute mark, say quietly: 'Your first answer is fine — move to the next one.' This is performance anxiety, not confusion.
Student finishes in under 5 minutesSay quietly: 'Go back and check. See if there's anything you want to add.' Note their name — resource for afternoon pairings.
Room goes quiet in a tense way (framing didn't land)Walk slowly through room. Say at normal volume: 'If anything is unfamiliar, not yet is the answer.' Resets tone for whole room.
Two or more students lean toward each other's papersWalk to table: 'This one is individual — your own answers only.' No further explanation. Move on.
Student refuses to workPlace sheet in front of them. Say quietly: 'You can write not yet on every single line — that is a complete answer, and it helps me.' Leave. Do not return until collection.
Hard Stop Do not extend time. One-minute warning at 0:12 is cut-off. Do not let students go to lunch without submitting sheets.
