Proportional reasoning
Two quantities are proportional if their ratio stays the same.
2 items = $6 → 5 items = $15 (always $3 per item)
To check: divide. If the answer is always the same, it is proportional.
Unit rate
A unit rate tells you how much of one thing happens per one of another thing. "Per 1 second," "per 1 mile," "per 1 dollar."
60 miles in 2 hours → 30 miles per 1 hour
To find a unit rate: divide the total by the number of units.
Ratio
A ratio compares two quantities. "3 to 2" means for every 3 of the first, there are 2 of the second.
3 blue : 2 red → 6 blue : 4 red → 9 blue : 6 red
Scale both sides by the same number to keep the ratio the same.
Linear decay
Something decreases by the same amount every time step.
100% − 5% per minute: 95, 90, 85, 80...
The drop is constant. Subtract the same amount each step.
Coordinate geometry

Proportional scaling
When you scale a recipe, map, or drawing up or down, every quantity is multiplied by the same factor. The ratios stay the same.
serves 4 → serves 12 is 3× → multiply every ingredient by 3
Arc approximation
A curved path can be estimated by breaking it into short straight segments and adding them up. The shorter the segments, the closer the estimate.
curve ≈ segment 1 + segment 2 + segment 3 + ...
Slope as rate of change
Slope tells you how much y changes for every 1 unit increase in x. It is a rate of change.
Slope = (change in y) ÷ (change in x)
Slope of 3: every time x goes up 1, y goes up 3. Negative slope = y is decreasing.
